# Draft Next Arc Options v28

Status: planning draft after `docs/DRAFT_NEXT_ARC_OPTIONS_v27.md`, updated after the
repo self-description seed was clarified as a separate but connected family direction.

This draft does not automatically supersede the contest-participation planning branch in
`docs/DRAFT_NEXT_ARC_OPTIONS_v26.md` or the structural-reasoning assessment planning
branch in `docs/DRAFT_NEXT_ARC_OPTIONS_v27.md`.

Instead, it records a third connected candidate family:

how should ADEU Studio emit a typed, generated self-description of the repo itself
before widening into recursive amendment, optimization entitlement, or constitutional
self-governance over vague prose summaries?

This is a planning document only. It is not a lock doc and does not authorize runtime
behavior, schema release, or implementation by itself.

  statements for this planning draft, not lock-equivalent permanent prohibitions by
  themselves;

## Gap

The repo still lacks a released way to:

- emit a typed descriptive model of its own current surfaces rather than relying mainly
  on prose atlases and maintainer continuity;
- classify repo entities across carrier kind, semantic role, governance posture,
  derivation posture, and mutation posture;
- classify schemas by role-form and authority posture using explicit precedence rules
  rather than filename families alone;
- surface test intent, dependency posture, and hotspot/consolidation signals as named
  descriptive objects;
- carry explicit epistemic posture for generated self-description outputs:
  observed, derived, inferred, adjudicated, and settled.

The missing layer is therefore not another constitutional policy note and not yet a
recursive amendment engine.

The missing layer is a descriptive self-model of ADEU itself.

## Relationship To `V43` And `V44`

`V43`, `V44`, and this new candidate family are connected but non-identical.

`V43` asks:

- what kind of external contest world is this?
- what is lawful?
- what is measured?
- where could reasoning AI help?

`V44` asks:

- is a candidate model structurally suitable to inhabit an explicit inferential
  skeleton under ADEU discipline?

This new family asks:

- what does the ADEU repo currently contain, how are those surfaces typed, and what is
  their epistemic and governance posture as a self-described artifact system?

So this family may constrain:

- future recursive amendment proposals;
- future repo optimization proposals;
- future atlas or decomposition automation;
- future meta-control surfaces that should operate over explicit descriptive objects.

But it may not mint:

- recursive amendment authority;
- constitutional override doctrine;
- optimization entitlement;
- contest or model-selection doctrine.

Planning relationship:

- `V43` remains a valid connected candidate family from `v26`;
- `V44` remains a valid connected candidate family from `v27`;
- this draft introduces a third connected candidate family rather than replacing either;
- whether `V45` should precede, run in parallel with, or later constrain other branches
  remains planning-bound and not yet locked.

## Closed Earlier Families And Surfaces

`V41` should now be read as a released practical repo-analysis substrate that proved ADEU
can analyze a bounded repo scope through request, settlement, observation, intended, and
alignment lanes.

The released `meta_*` surfaces should now be read as early constitutional and
meta-control scaffolding rather than a whole-repo descriptive self-model.

Neither `V41` nor the released `meta_*` family emitted a generated ADEU-native catalog
of the repo itself as a current artifact system.

`V45` is the planning move that generalizes the descriptive self-model thread without
reopening practical-analysis or recursive-governance releases.

## Recommended Next Family (`V45`)

`V45` should release a bounded descriptive doctrine around:

- repo entity catalogs;
- schema family registries;
- explicit extraction provenance and epistemic posture;
- explicit multi-axis entity taxonomy;
- typed dependency and test-intent visibility;
- typed hotspot and consolidation diagnostics;
- explicit distinction between:
  - descriptive finding,
  - optimization candidate,
  - amendment entitlement.
- explicit promotion law for descriptive posture transitions:
  - inferred -> adjudicated;
  - adjudicated -> settled.

The family should treat recursive-governance or amendment logic as:

- downstream consumers of the descriptive outputs;
- not the first release inside this family.

The family should treat existing meta-control surfaces as:

- constitutionally relevant context;
- not the artifact family being selected here.

The classification-promotion law should not remain implicit.

At minimum, later `V45` work should make explicit:

- who or what may promote an inferred classification to adjudicated posture;
- what evidence is sufficient for that promotion;
- who or what may promote an adjudicated classification to settled posture;
- whether different entity classes may use different promotion rules.

## Recommended Next Path (`V45-A`)

Implement the bounded repo entity-catalog and schema-family-registry lane first.

`V45-A` should introduce:

- one canonical bounded repo entity catalog candidate artifact:
  - explicit repo-visible snapshot scope;
  - explicit extraction provenance;
  - explicit multi-axis entity taxonomy over at least:
    - surface kind;
    - semantic role;
    - governance posture;
    - derivation posture;
    - mutation posture;
  - explicit epistemic posture over emitted classifications:
    - observed;
    - derived deterministically;
    - inferred heuristically;
    - adjudicated;
    - settled;
- one canonical bounded schema family registry candidate artifact:
  - explicit schema family membership;
  - explicit role-form classification;
  - explicit role-form precedence across:
    - structural signature;
    - semantic function;
    - governance role;
    - lexical naming;
  - explicit confidence or adjudication posture when structure alone does not settle the
    role;
- one bounded reference snapshot or fixture posture over the ADEU repo;
- no widening yet into mutation proposals, recursive amendment logic, or optimization
  prescriptions.

`V45-A` is descriptive-first and non-promotional:

- it may emit bounded descriptive catalogs and diagnostics;
- it may not yet emit mutation entitlement, recursive amendment entitlement, or
  optimization prescriptions.

## Why This Path

- It is the narrowest safe consumer of the repo self-description seed.
- It turns the current prose-atlas posture into typed self-description without jumping
  into mutation authority.
- It establishes extraction trust and classification posture before broader symbol,
  test, or optimization surfaces are added.
- It creates the explicit object layer that later recursive-governance lanes would need
  if they are ever selected.
- It prevents the family from collapsing immediately into a stealth refactor queue or
  self-amendment engine.

## First-Slice Boundary (`V45-A`)

`V45-A` should stay bounded to:

- repo entity catalog definition only;
- schema family registry definition only;
- explicit snapshot and stale-snapshot semantics only;
- explicit extraction provenance and confidence posture only;
- bounded deterministic or adjudicated classification posture only;
- one bounded repo snapshot or fixture surface only.

It should not attempt:

- broad symbol-level overlap mining;
- broad test-intent inference;
- optimization-register release;
- recursive amendment logic;
- mutation recommendation or refactor entitlement;
- constitutional override or self-modification doctrine;
- automatic repo mutation.
