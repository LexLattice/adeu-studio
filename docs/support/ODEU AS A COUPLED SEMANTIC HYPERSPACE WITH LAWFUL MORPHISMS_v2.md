## 1. Framing and retained backbone

The retained backbone stands. ODEU is still one coupled semantic object with four irreducible regimes of force: O for world-structure, E for warrant/challenge/suspension, D for binding force, and U for preference/optimization. The familiar cross remains a true first chart, but only a chart. ODEU also remains the last semantic membrane before bounded downstream adjudication, not a replacement for Boolean, probabilistic, deontic, or optimization engines. 

The hardening move is therefore not to replace the hyperspace model, but to place it more exactly inside the repo’s membrane doctrine and authority doctrine: ODEU is the internal semantic geometry used by the membrane to track coupled bundle states, route non-closure lawfully, and prepare bounded downstream projections.  

### Relation to ODEU_MEMBRANE_ARCHITECTURE

Let the upstream semantic field be

[
s=\langle \sigma,\omega,\kappa,\alpha,\tau,h\rangle
]

with source expressions (\sigma), world-linked material (\omega), discourse context (\kappa), authority frame (\alpha), task horizon (\tau), and harvested priors (h). Let the upstream proposal office emit a candidate decomposition (\delta_i) at pass (i). The membrane checkpoint at pass (i) is then modeled as

[
M_i=\langle X_i,R_i,J_i,\Xi_i,N_i,F_i\rangle
]

where (X_i\subseteq \mathcal X) is the finite tracked-bundle set, (R_i) the residual register, (J_i) the rejection log, (\Xi_i) the escalation queue, (N_i) the reproposal demand set, and (F_i\subseteq X_i) the frozen accepted core. A recomposed settlement is a distinct object,

[
G_i=\mathsf{Settle}(M_i),
]

not a point of (\mathcal X). 

So the exact answer to “what is (x\in\mathcal X)?” is:

> (x) is one **tracked coupled bundle state** at a membrane checkpoint.

It is **not** the whole checkpoint. It is **not** the whole recomposed settlement. It becomes an **accepted coupled packet bundle** only when its membrane-routing status is accepted. This is the least confusing relation between the retained hyperspace and the membrane architecture.  

The base locus (b\in B) is not an arbitrary coordinate. It is the membrane-internal, minimally compiled task-context locus extracted from the semantic field:

[
b=(\mathrm{subj},\mathrm{scope},\mathrm{time},\mathrm{jur},\kappa,\alpha,\tau).
]

Source spans (\sigma) do not become locus coordinates; they remain source anchors inside witnesses and lineage. Thus (B) is the space of bounded semantic situations under compiled subject, scope, time, authority frame, and task frame. 

Lane packets and coupled bundles relate by projection and coupling. For a tracked bundle (x), let (\pi_O(x),\pi_E(x),\pi_D(x),\pi_U(x)) be its lane packets. Then the accepted lane packet sets at checkpoint (i) are

[
Q_L^i={\pi_L(x)\mid x\in X_i,\ \sigma_{\text{port}}(x)=\mathrm{accepted}}.
]

A coupled bundle is not just a 4-tuple of packets. It is lane packets plus coupling graph plus witness chain plus lineage and status. The membrane can therefore accept some bundles, residualize others, reject others, and escalate others without pretending that the whole checkpoint is one state. 

The membrane-status words in the retained note now receive exact routing meaning. A local `suspended` state is ordinarily routed to residualization unless a bounded local closure policy applies. A local `blocked` state produces either a residual entry plus reproposal demand, or a rejection entry if the crossing attempt is illicit by form. A local `escalated` state produces a typed queue entry whose target is an office, not an abstract “higher level.” A `ready[Π_k]` mark means the bundle is accepted, witness-closed enough for the footprint of (\Pi_k), and admissible for bounded downstream export.  

Finally, the hyperspace is not static. It participates in recursive semantic compilation:

[
\delta_i=\Sigma^\uparrow(s,r_i,h_i),\qquad
M_i=\mathsf{Mem}(s,\delta_i,h_i),\qquad
r_{i+1}=\mathsf{Reprop}(M_i),\qquad
h_{i+1}=\mathsf{Harvest}(h_i,\mathrm{trace}_i).
]

That is: proposal, membrane checkpointing, structured reproposal, and harvest are adjacent process layers around the ODEU geometry. The geometry is the internal law-space of coupled bundle states inside that loop.

## 2. Choice of formal object

The minimal formal object remains the same class as in the retained note, with one narrow hardening:

[
\mathfrak{X}
============

\bigl(
p:\mathcal{X}\to B,\ \mathrm{Strat},\ \mathcal{M},\ \mathbb{W}^{\mathrm{chain}},\ \mathbb{A},\ \Pi
\bigr).
]

ODEU is still best described as a **witness-guarded stratified fibered state-space with partial typed morphisms**. The hardening is only that the witness system is now explicitly a **witness-chain system**, not merely an unstructured local bundle.  

This remains the weakest adequate object.

A plain typed graph is still too weak because it does not naturally distinguish local mixed loading, dominance zones, routing status, or path-sensitive admissibility. A full membrane transducer is too broad because it would collapse the internal semantic geometry into the whole compilation architecture. A manifold remains too strong and too vague. A pure sheaf-like picture remains too underpowered for guarded transition law. A full categorical overhaul remains heavier than necessary because what is really needed is guarded partial composition, not free abstract composition.  

The exact fit is therefore:

* the **membrane architecture** is the surrounding checkpoint-and-reentry process,
* the **ODEU hyperspace** is the internal state geometry over tracked coupled bundles,
* the **downstream calculi** are partial projections from admissible regions of that hyperspace.

That separation is important because it preserves the claim that ODEU is a membrane-law field, not the whole compiler and not the whole execution stack.

## 3. The 2D chart/projection and what it preserves

The pedagogical ODEU cross remains a chart of the richer space, not the territory. The chart is defined on tracked bundle states (x\in\mathcal X), not on raw entangled field material outside the membrane. That distinction matters because the chart presupposes at least enough compilation to produce lane observables.  

Define derived lane observables

[
\rho_O,\rho_E,\rho_D,\rho_U:\mathcal X\to \mathbb R_{\ge 0},
]

and then the chart

[
\chi(x)=\bigl(\chi_{OE}(x),\chi_{DU}(x)\bigr)
]

with

[
\chi_{OE}(x)=\frac{\rho_O(x)-\rho_E(x)}{\rho_O(x)+\rho_E(x)+\varepsilon},
\qquad
\chi_{DU}(x)=\frac{\rho_D(x)-\rho_U(x)}{\rho_D(x)+\rho_U(x)+\varepsilon}.
]

As before, this preserves first-order oppositional structure: O-versus-E and D-versus-U dominance. It preserves the fact that the visible quadrants are dominance zones, not isolated boxes. It preserves why O↔E and D↔U are the privileged displayed pairings. 

What it collapses is now even clearer after hardening: office allocation, delegation, authority succession, witness-chain status, replay requirements, residualization vs rejection, escalation targets, harvest lineage, and the difference between a path that is merely surface-similar and a path that is lawfully equivalent. Those are real features of the hyperspace and membrane loop, but they do not survive the 2D shadow.  

The center remains the zone of entangled live material prior to full lane separation. In hardened form, that is not merely “low dominance”; it often correlates with a nonempty separability-defect profile (\eta), meaning unresolved lane coupling, scope conflict, authority unbinding, or witness insufficiency. The chart remains useful because it displays first-order tension. It remains incomplete because it cannot display why the tension exists or what lawful next moves are available.

## 4. Internal structure of O, E, D, U

### O: ontology / world-structure

The retained O-lane stays:

[
o=(G_O,\Xi_O,T_O,P_O).
]

(G_O) is a typed object/state graph, (\Xi_O) an incompatibility relation, (T_O) an ontic transition relation, and (P_O) a persistence/change map. This still captures candidate world-state, ontic objecthood, absence, contradiction, persistence, and change. The retained distinction also stands: ontic absence belongs to O; epistemic unknown belongs to E. 

### E: epistemics / witness-bearing assessment

The retained E-lane also stands:

[
e=(C_E,S_E,A_E,P_E,K_E,B_E,\Sigma_E).
]

(C_E) holds claims/hypotheses/questions, (S_E) support, (A_E) attack/falsification, (P_E) provenance and source-quality structure, (K_E) confidence geometry, (B_E) counterfactual branches, and (\Sigma_E) epistemic posture. The useful confidence geometry remains non-scalar:

[
K_E(c)=(\mathrm{sup},\mathrm{att},\mathrm{res}),
]

so insufficiency, contradiction, and defeasible support stay distinct. The hardening adds that provenance state (P_E) now also carries witness-fragment refs and effective intervals suitable for chain-status propagation.  

### D: deontics / binding force, now with office calculus

This is the main hardening. The D-lane is no longer merely “norms plus authority as a verbal tag.” A local D-state is now

[
d=(N_D,G_D,\preceq_D,\Theta_D,\preceq_\Theta,\mathrm{Occ}_D,\mathrm{Del}_D,Exc_D,Esc_D,Sup_D).
]

Here (N_D) is the norm set, (G_D) gates, (\preceq_D) deontic precedence, (\Theta_D) the office set, (\preceq_\Theta) office precedence, (\mathrm{Occ}_D) office occupancy/allocation relation, (\mathrm{Del}_D) delegation relation, (Exc_D) exception structure, (Esc_D) escalation structure, and (Sup_D) revocation/supersession structure. This imports the consumer-vs-office distinction, delegation law, and status-sensitive authority handling into ODEU without turning D into a full political constitution. 

Each norm now has an office-real source:

[
n=(\mathrm{mod},\mathrm{cond},\mathrm{scope},\mathrm{target},\theta_{\mathrm{src}},\mathrm{prio},\mathrm{state}),
]

where (\theta_{\mathrm{src}}\in\Theta_D) is the source office or office-basis handle.

Each office object is minimally

[
\theta=
(\mathrm{id},\mathrm{scope},
R^{ord},R^{exc},R^{sup},R^{esc},R^{del},R^{nd},
\beta,\mathrm{interval},\mathrm{succ}),
]

where (R^{ord}) are ordinary applicability rights, (R^{exc}) exception/override rights, (R^{sup}) revocation/supersession/re-grounding rights, (R^{esc}) escalation-routing rights, (R^{del}) delegable rights, (R^{nd}) nondelegable rights, (\beta) the authority basis, (\mathrm{interval}) the effective interval, and (\mathrm{succ}) the succession relation. 

Occupancy is not inspection:

[
\mathrm{Occ}(a,\theta,t)
\iff
\mathrm{allocationCert}(a,\theta,t)\land \mathrm{basisValid}(\theta,t).
]

So the hard non-equivalence law is:

[
\mathrm{Consumer}(a,\text{public law}) \not\Rightarrow \mathrm{Occ}(a,\theta,t).
]

A person or system may inspect public law without holding any deontic office under it. This is the minimal import of the vertical consumer/office distinction into ODEU. 

Delegation is not implicit. It must satisfy:

[
\mathrm{Del}(\theta\to\theta',t)
\iff
Rights(\theta')\subseteq R^{del}(\theta)
\land
scope(\theta')\subseteq scope(\theta)
\land
interval(\theta')\subseteq interval(\theta)
\land
Rights(\theta')\cap R^{nd}(\theta)=\varnothing
\land
\omega^{auth}_{del}\ \text{exists}.
]

This is the bounded office law needed for override, exception, escalation, revocation, and supersession to be office-real rather than rhetoric-real. 

The key distinction inside D is now exact:

* **Ordinary norm applicability** is the activation of an already valid norm under evidenced conditions.
* **Exception, override, scope broadening, revocation, supersession, and re-grounding** are office acts and require occupied offices with the specific corresponding rights.

So E may bind an existing norm by proving its antecedent, but E may not create that norm, broaden its scope, revoke it, or override it. Those are D acts through offices.  

Escalation targets are typed offices, not abstract destinations:

[
Esc_D:N_D \rightharpoonup 2^{\Theta_D}.
]

A lawful escalation therefore points to a specific office class or office instance competent for the unresolved issue. 

Interaction between deontic precedence and office precedence is computed on pairs, not norms alone. Define effective precedence by

[
(n_1,\theta_1)\succeq_{\mathrm{eff}}(n_2,\theta_2)
]

only when the scopes overlap and either both ((n_1\succeq_D n_2)) and ((\theta_1\succeq_\Theta \theta_2)) hold, or an explicit override bridge witness authorizes the exception. A high office does not automatically rewrite norm law, and a high-priority norm does not automatically bypass office incompetence. 

### U: utility / preference / strategy

The retained U-lane stands:

[
u=(G_U^{loc},G_U^{glob},\preceq_U,V_U,\mathcal F_U,T_U).
]

Local/global goals, preference preorder, value model, feasible strategy family, and tradeoff structure remain the right minimal contents. The hardening adds only one bounded exactness clause: when U is an institutional objective surface rather than a private preference surface, reprioritization or tradeoff rewrites may require a goal-setting office or policy source. That still does not transform U into D. It merely says collective objective-setting must itself be sourced.

## 5. Formal representation of semantic states

A tracked semantic bundle state is now

[
x=
\langle
c,b,o,e,d,u,\Lambda,W,h,\rho,\eta,\sigma
\rangle.
]

Here (c) is the checkpoint ref, (b) the compiled locus, (o,e,d,u) the lane states, (\Lambda) the typed coupling graph, (W) the witness chain, (h) the lineage/dependency trace, (\rho) the lane observables, (\eta) the separability-defect profile, and (\sigma) the status record. This preserves the retained packet idea while making checkpoint placement and witness-chain dependence explicit.  

The coupling graph (\Lambda) remains the place where the actual cross-lane field is realized: `about`, `supports`, `refutes`, `justifies`, `gates`, `excepts`, `serves_goal`, `prioritizes`, `instantiates`, and `predicts` remain canonical coupling kinds. The hardening adds that office relations are also part of coupling structure where relevant, such as `occupied_by`, `delegated_from`, `escalates_to`, and `superseded_by`.  

The witness component is no longer only a flat bundle. Write

[
W=(\omega,\mathcal C_W,\zeta_W),
]

where (\omega) is the active local frontier bundle for this state, (\mathcal C_W) the dependency chain or DAG behind that frontier, and (\zeta_W) its status flags. This makes room for witness reuse, witness supersession, and replay law. 

The separability-defect term (\eta) is now explicitly typed. It is not a generic scalar uncertainty. Use it as a finite defect profile, for example

[
\eta\subseteq
{
\texttt{alias_unresolved},
\texttt{boundary_ambiguous},
\texttt{scope_conflict},
\texttt{authority_unbound},
\texttt{objective_underdetermined},
\texttt{lane_mixed},
\texttt{provenance_gap}
}.
]

A scalar severity may still be derived for charting or queueing, but the doctrinal object is the typed defect set. That is the exact way to make entanglement/separability a lawful condition instead of decorative rhetoric. 

The status record is decomposed into four components:

[
\sigma=(\sigma_{loc},\sigma_{port},\sigma_{chain},\sigma_{proj}).
]

Local semantic status:

[
\sigma_{loc}\in
{
\mathrm{open},
\mathrm{suspended},
\mathrm{blocked},
\mathrm{escalated},
\mathrm{settled}
}.
]

Membrane-routing status:

[
\sigma_{port}\in
{
\mathrm{tracked},
\mathrm{accepted},
\mathrm{residualized},
\mathrm{rejected},
\mathrm{queued}
}.
]

Witness-chain status flags:

[
\sigma_{chain}\subseteq
{
\mathrm{active},
\mathrm{stale},
\mathrm{superseded},
\mathrm{needs_replay},
\mathrm{invalid}
}.
]

Projection status flags:

[
\sigma_{proj}\subseteq
{
\mathrm{ready}[\Pi_B],
\mathrm{ready}[\Pi_P],
\mathrm{ready}[\Pi_D],
\mathrm{ready}[\Pi_U],
\mathrm{projected}[\Pi_B],
\mathrm{projected}[\Pi_P],
\mathrm{projected}[\Pi_D],
\mathrm{projected}[\Pi_U]
}.
]

This is the cleanest way to align the retained state model to checkpoint routing, replay law, and downstream readiness without flattening them into one status word.   

Checkpoint and settlement are then:

[
M_c=\langle X_c,R_c,J_c,\Xi_c,N_c,F_c\rangle,
\qquad
G_c=\mathsf{Settle}(M_c).
]

Accepted bundles are the accepted port stratum

[
X_c^{acc}={x\in X_c\mid \sigma_{port}(x)=\mathrm{accepted}}.
]

Lane packet sets are projections of that accepted stratum:

[
Q_L^c={\pi_L(x)\mid x\in X_c^{acc}}.
]

This makes the relation between lane packets and coupled bundle states exact. 

A serializable shape is therefore not just the earlier packet plus dominance scores; it also needs checkpoint refs, office refs, defect profile, and chain status. The retained packet model survives, but it is now checkpoint-aware and replay-aware.

## 6. Morphism families

The retained family catalogue stands. The hardening here does not replace the original morphism taxonomy; it adds exact guard clauses, office conditions, and witness-chain consequences. A morphism instance is still written

[
x \xRightarrow[\alpha]{\mu:\omega} y,
]

but now (\omega) is understood as the active frontier bundle of a larger witness chain (W), and (\alpha) includes office context where relevant. The guard remains

[
G_\mu(x,\omega,\alpha)\in
{\mathrm{admit},\mathrm{suspend},\mathrm{escalate},\mathrm{block}},
]

with membrane routing handled separately by (\sigma_{port}). 

### O-internal families

The retained O families remain:

[
\mathrm{ref}_O,\ \mathrm{rev}_O,\ \mathrm{excl}_O,\ \mathrm{abs}_O,\ \mathrm{conc}_O,\ \mathrm{step}_O.
]

Their source/target conditions and preservation laws remain as previously stated. The only hardening is that when an O-update writes an institutional or official state rather than a merely local model state, the transition must carry the relevant authority witness and office basis. A local refinement is not automatically an official record mutation.  

### E-internal families

The retained E families remain:

[
\mathrm{acc}_E,\ \mathrm{fals}_E,\ \mathrm{weak}_E,\ \mathrm{susp}_E,\ \mathrm{prov}_E^{\uparrow/\downarrow},\ \mathrm{cf}_E,\ \mathrm{merge}_E.
]

The hardening is that provenance upgrade/downgrade, source retraction, freshness loss, or dependency collapse may not merely alter support posture; they may also change witness-chain status. In particular, a provenance downgrade can mark later descendants `stale` or `needs_replay` even when the visible claim token is unchanged.  

### D-internal families

The retained D families remain:

[
\mathrm{bind}_D,\ \mathrm{scope}_D^{\downarrow/\uparrow},\ \mathrm{rev}_D,\ \mathrm{sup}_D,\ \mathrm{exc}_D,\ \mathrm{esc}_D,\ \mathrm{regr}_D.
]

Here the hardening is substantial.

(\mathrm{bind}_D) is still ordinary norm activation: it applies an already valid norm to a case once its antecedent is witnessed. It does **not** require a fresh override or promulgation act. It does require that the norm’s office-basis is active for the current scope.

(\mathrm{scope}_D^\downarrow) can often be executed by the same or a lower-competence office when the public law permits narrowing. (\mathrm{scope}_D^\uparrow) is stricter: broadening scope requires a competent office right, not just a modeling choice.

(\mathrm{rev}_D,\mathrm{sup}_D,\mathrm{regr}_D) are office-real acts. They require occupied offices with the specific rights to revoke, supersede, or re-ground. Re-grounding is especially important when the source office changes by succession while content remains partly stable.

(\mathrm{exc}_D) requires either an auto-authorized exception already encoded in the parent norm or an occupied office carrying exception/override rights. Utility pressure alone is never enough.

(\mathrm{esc}_D) routes to typed offices in (Esc_D). A lawful escalation is therefore witnessed both by trigger conditions and by a valid target office.  

### U-internal families

The retained U families remain:

[
\mathrm{repr}_U,\ \mathrm{rew}_U,\ \mathrm{ev}_U,\ \mathrm{opt}_U^B,\ \mathrm{branch}_U,\ \mathrm{trade}_U.
]

The hardening clause is narrow: if the utility surface is collective or institutional rather than private, reprioritization, reweighting, or tradeoff transformation may require a declared objective-setting source. That source can be office-backed without turning U into D. The content remains utility content. 

### Cross-lane families

The retained cross-lane families also stand:

[
\mathrm{form}*{O\to E},\
\mathrm{commit}*{E\to O},\
\mathrm{bind}*{E\to D},\
\mathrm{prop}*{E\to D},\
\mathrm{constr}*{D\to U},\
\mathrm{pet}*{U\to D},\
\mathrm{eval}*{O\to U},\
\mathrm{target}*{U\to O},\
\mathrm{upd}*{E\to U},\
\mathrm{inq}*{U\to E},\
\mathrm{inst}*{O\to D},\
\mathrm{env}*{D\to O}.
]

The hardening clauses are constitutional:

(\mathrm{bind}_{E\to D}) binds **existing** norms only. It proves antecedent satisfaction; it does not mint deontic force.

(\mathrm{prop}_{E\to D}) remains proposal only until a competent office acts.

(\mathrm{constr}_{D\to U}) remains the gating law: D shapes the admissible utility search region.

(\mathrm{pet}_{U\to D}) remains petition only. It may create a request, not a rule.

(\mathrm{target}_{U\to O}) remains candidate target construction only. Desired futures do not become actual world-states by preference.

(\mathrm{env}_{D\to O}) annotates admissibility, not actuality.

These are unchanged in spirit from the retained note, but now office typing makes them exact.  

### Boundary operators

Ingress, residualization, rejection, reproposal, escalation routing, and harvest are not ordinary internal morphisms in (\mathcal M). They are membrane boundary operators surrounding the hyperspace. They will be formalized in section 9. This separation preserves the claim that ODEU is the internal semantic geometry of the membrane, not the whole membrane object.

## 7. Witness-bearing transition law

The retained witness-bundle doctrine stands, but it must now be read as the frontier of a witness chain.

Let the local frontier bundle remain

[
\omega=
(
\omega^{ev},
\omega^{prov},
\omega^{auth},
\omega^{der},
\omega^{scope},
\omega^{sup},
\omega^{cf},
\omega^{exc}
).
]

The hardening is that a lawful transition depends not only on this frontier bundle, but also on the supporting chain behind it. Write the full witness chain as

[
W=(F,\triangleleft,\mathrm{cov},\zeta),
]

where (F) is the fragment set, (\triangleleft) the dependency relation, (\mathrm{cov}) the obligation-coverage map, and (\zeta) the chain-status flags. Each fragment is minimally

[
f=
(\mathrm{id},\mathrm{kind},\mathrm{subj},\mathrm{premiseRefs},
\mathrm{covers},\mathrm{mode},\mathrm{interval},\mathrm{status}).
]

This imports witness composition and status propagation directly into ODEU transition law.  

A transition is now lawful only if

[
\mathrm{Lawful}*\mu(x,W,\alpha)
\iff
\mathrm{TypeOK}*\mu(W)
\land
\mathrm{ProvOK}(W)
\land
\mathrm{ScopeOK}(x,W)
\land
\mathrm{AuthOK}*\mu(\alpha,W)
\land
\mathrm{DeriveOK}*\mu(x,W)
\land
\mathrm{NoStrongerThanWitness}_\mu(x,W,\alpha).
]

The retained anti-laundering law therefore remains, but coverage is now computed over the active chain, not just the visible frontier:

[
\kappa(y\mid x)\preceq \mathrm{cov}(W,\alpha).
]

A transition may not end broader in scope, stronger in certainty, stronger in modality, or stronger in authority than its active witness chain and office context can carry. 

### Witness composition

Two successive transitions compose lawfully only when their witness chains compose. If

[
x \xRightarrow[\alpha_1]{\mu_1:\omega_1} y
\quad\text{and}\quad
y \xRightarrow[\alpha_2]{\mu_2:\omega_2} z,
]

then the witness chains (W_1,W_2) are composable only if their support maps and obligation coverage are compatible, their material premises do not contradict one another, and no material fragment in the composed chain is stale or invalid. This is the ODEU-local version of witness composition calculus. 

### Witness fragment reuse

A fragment may be reused only when the legally material identity of the thing proved has not changed. Reuse requires sameness of subject commitment, sameness of material premises or lawfully bridged successors, sameness of discharged obligation kind, same or stronger discharge mode, active office/verifier basis, and still-valid effective interval. That is why scope witnesses or office-allocation witnesses may often be reused across several case-local transitions, while binding or conformance witnesses are usually narrower. 

### Reissue conditions

Reissue is required when any material legality-bearing parameter changes: target commitment, scope, authority basis, office occupancy, delegation basis, proof/discharge mode, effective interval, or an upstream witness on which the fragment materially depends. In practical terms: a visible source and target may look unchanged while the transition becomes non-equivalent because the witness chain changed under them. 

### Equivalence for legality purposes

Two witness bundles or chains are equivalent for legality purposes only when they discharge the same material obligations for the same target commitment under the same or stronger modes, over the same material scope, with the same active authority basis or lawfully bridged successor basis. Surface similarity is not enough.

Write that as

[
W \approx_{\mathrm{leg}} W'
]

for legality-equivalence. Then two transitions with the same visible source and target are still not lawfully equivalent if (W\not\approx_{\mathrm{leg}}W'). 

### Status handling and replay

Witness-chain status is semantically real. The core flags are:

* `stale`: interval expired, freshness degraded, or source downgraded without hard defeat;
* `superseded`: a successor basis exists for some material fragment;
* `needs_replay`: a downstream state still depends on changed material fragments and must be recomputed;
* `invalid`: hard revocation, authority defect, failed binding, or stronger defeating basis.

Replay is required when

[
\mathrm{ReplayReq}(y)
\iff
\exists f\in \mathrm{dep}(y):
\mathrm{material}(f,y)\land \mathrm{changed}(f).
]

This is the exact point where replay/supersession becomes part of the semantics rather than post hoc implementation bookkeeping.

## 8. Illicit laundering vs lawful transition

The retained illicit-laundering doctrine stands, now with office and witness-chain exactness.

An apparent transition (x\leadsto y) is illicit if either no lawful morphism family and witness chain license it, or the visible move is treated as closed even though its chain is stale, superseded, unbound, or invalid. Formally:

[
\mathrm{Launder}(x\leadsto y)
\iff
\neg\exists \mu,W,\alpha\
\mathrm{Lawful}_\mu(x,W,\alpha)\ \text{with target } y
]
or the move is treated as active despite disqualifying chain status.  

The canonical retained laundering cases remain:

1. U directly minting D.
2. Weak E masquerading as settled O.
3. D retrofitted from convenience.
4. O sent downstream without E mediation.
5. Exception inflated into standing norm.
6. Override invoked without a real office.
7. U-target construction treated as actuality.
8. Counterfactual branch imported into actuality. 

The hardening adds four more exact illicit forms.

**Stale-witness laundering.** A stale or superseded witness fragment is reused as if current and equivalent.

**Office-laundering.** A public consumer, or an actor with the wrong office, is treated as if they hold exception, override, revocation, or supersession rights.

**Replay-skipping.** A downstream commitment built on changed material premises is treated as still operative without replay.

**Harvest-self-promotion.** A harvested morph template, admissibility prior, or repeated success trace is treated as authority or live rule by itself. Harvest can condition future search; it cannot self-authenticate force.   

The doctrine therefore tightens to:

> No lane, witness surface, office surface, or harvested template may export stronger semantic force than its lawful chain and authority basis can carry.

## 9. Composition, reversibility, supersession, and path dependence

The retained doctrine of guarded partial composition remains correct. If

[
x \xRightarrow[\alpha_1]{\mu_1:\omega_1} y
\quad\text{and}\quad
y \xRightarrow[\alpha_2]{\mu_2:\omega_2} z,
]

then composition is lawful only when source/target signatures match, scopes compose, unresolved suspension/escalation is not skipped, and the later witness chain does not silently invalidate material premises of the earlier one except through an explicit revision or supersession act. Order still matters. Path still matters. Surface-equivalence still differs from lawful equivalence. 

Reversibility also remains witness-bearing rather than magical. Refinement can be partially reversed by abstraction. Evidential support can be weakened. O-commitments can be revised. D bindings can be withdrawn by expiry, revocation, or supersession. U choices can be recomputed under new D or E. But those reversals are lawful only through chain-bearing transitions, not by erasure. 

### Supersession and propagation

If (r) is a material authority, witness, norm, or derivation basis for state (x), then changes to (r) propagate to descendants. Let (\mathrm{dep}(x)) be the material dependency set. Then material upstream change yields replay pressure:

[
r\in \mathrm{dep}(x)\land \mathrm{changed}(r)\ \Rightarrow\ x\ \text{becomes stale, superseded, needs_replay, or invalid}.
]

This is the ODEU-internal counterpart of status propagation in the vertical alignment doctrine. It is not optional bookkeeping. It determines whether the state remains fit to serve as a live semantic basis. 

### Ingress from entangled semantic material

Ingress is a membrane-boundary operator, not an internal lane morphism:

[
\mathsf{Ingress}_i:(s,\delta_i)\rightharpoonup M_i.
]

Its function is to compile entangled field material into tracked bundle states, residuals, rejections, escalation entries, and reproposal demands. Lane separation may be incomplete. That incompleteness is represented by (\eta), not hidden. 

### Residualization

Residualization is a lawful outcome, not mere failure. A bundle should be residualized when it is semantically meaningful but not yet lawfully closable for the current footprint. Typical causes are unresolved scope, unresolved referent identity, insufficient provenance, incomplete office binding, or unresolved lane entanglement. In that case:

[
x\ \text{with}\ \sigma_{loc}\in{\mathrm{suspended},\mathrm{blocked}}
\quad\leadsto\quad
r(x)\in R_i
]

together with reason code, required repair, and frozen accepted substructures if any. This preserves semantic work rather than collapsing it to false or discarding it. 

### Rejection

Rejection is distinct from residualization. Rejection is appropriate when the attempted crossing is illicit by form: for example raw U-as-D laundering, source with no admissible provenance anchor where one is constitutively required, or a direct export of unresolved material as Boolean settlement. Rejection goes to (J_i), not (R_i). That distinction prevents the membrane from treating semantic illegality and ordinary under-resolution as the same thing. 

### Structured reproposal and re-entry

Blocked or residualized states should generate structured reproposal demands, not generic “try again.” A minimal reproposal demand is

[
n=
(\mathrm{blocking_lane},
\mathrm{reason_code},
\mathrm{required_probe},
\mathrm{required_retyping},
F_i,
\mathrm{prohibited_rewrites},
\theta_{esc}?,
\mathrm{priority}).
]

This aligns the hyperspace with recursive veto-and-reproposal architecture: accepted substructures are frozen, forbidden rewrites are explicit, and the upstream office is told what kind of witness, scope repair, office binding, or probe is actually needed. 

### Freeze law

Accepted substructures are not re-opened casually. If (F_i\subseteq X_i^{acc}) is the frozen accepted core at pass (i), then later reproposals must preserve (F_i) unless an explicit revision, supersession, or invalidation witness authorizes reopening. This gives the recursive loop memory and prevents semantic drift. 

### Harvest

Repeated successful traces, or repeated structured failure traces, may be harvested:

[
h_{i+1}=\mathsf{Harvest}(h_i,\mathrm{trace}_i).
]

What is harvested are reusable decomposition patterns, admissibility priors, morph templates, replay recipes, escalation templates, and probe plans. What is **not** harvested is fresh authority. Harvested templates remain priors or playbooks unless separately promoted through a lawful channel. That is why harvest improves future reproposal and routing without becoming semantic laundering.

## 10. Membrane boundary to downstream adjudication

ODEU remains the last semantic preprocessing membrane before bounded downstream calculi. The hardening only makes readiness more exact.

A downstream projection (\Pi_k) is partial:

[
\Pi_k:R_k\subseteq \mathcal X \to Y_k.
]

A state is ready for (\Pi_k) only if

[
R_k(x)
\iff
\sigma_{loc}(x)=\mathrm{settled}
\land
\sigma_{port}(x)=\mathrm{accepted}
\land
\mathrm{active}\in \sigma_{chain}(x)
\land
\mathrm{needs_replay}\notin \sigma_{chain}(x)
\land
\mathrm{invalid}\notin \sigma_{chain}(x)
\land
\mathrm{LaneSep}_k(x)
\land
\mathrm{WitnessClosed}_k(x)
\land
\mathrm{ScopeClosed}_k(x)
\land
\mathrm{AuthorityClosed}_k(x)
\land
\mathrm{AmbClosed}_k(x).
]

This is the hardened version of the retained readiness law. Residualized, rejected, queued, stale-without-closure, or replay-pending states do not cross as if settled.   

The retained downstream projections remain correct.

For Boolean closure:

[
\Pi_B:R_B\to{0,1},
]

mainly over O/E once subject, scope, and support/attack posture are lawfully closed.

For probabilistic estimation:

[
\Pi_P:R_P\to\text{intervals or distributions},
]

again mainly over O/E, with bounded evidence model and counterfactual assumptions.

For deontic verdict:

[
\Pi_D:R_D\to
{\mathrm{required},\mathrm{forbidden},\mathrm{permitted},\mathrm{escalate},\mathrm{blocked}},
]

over O/E/D, now with office closure and exception/override competence explicitly resolved.

For utility selection:

[
\Pi_U(x)=\arg\max_{a\in A_{\mathrm{adm}}(x)}V_U(a\mid e_x)
]

or a bounded frontier over the D-filtered admissible action set (A_{\mathrm{adm}}(x)). 

The membrane doctrine remains unchanged in substance: upstream belong claim formation, witness qualification, provenance binding, authority binding, applicability analysis, exception/escalation analysis, candidate generation, ambiguity classification, and replay/supersession handling. Downstream belong bounded closure and selection procedures. No downstream engine mints missing authority, missing witnesses, or hidden semantic rewrites. If those are needed, control returns upstream.

## 11. Worked examples

### Example 1 — factual claim under falsification pressure

**Initial mixed state.** At checkpoint (c_0), bundle (x_0) concerns valve (V12). O carries candidate state `open(V12)`. E contains one pressure-sensor support item with moderate provenance. D contains a safety rule gating remote actuation under state uncertainty. U prefers uninterrupted flow. The bundle is tracked, not yet settled.

**Lawful path.** (O\to E) first forms an assessable claim. Sensor evidence accumulates. Camera evidence and later local inspection attack the initial claim. A calibration audit downgrades the sensor provenance. That downgrade changes not only the E posture but also the witness-chain status: the chain becomes `stale` with respect to the earlier remote-open inference. Because the conflict is real and unresolved, the local state becomes `suspended` and the port status becomes `residualized`. The reproposal demand is: obtain direct state witness or a stronger admissible instrument witness. After local inspection, replay occurs; the chain becomes active again; (E\to O) revises the O-state to `closed(V12)`.  

**Blocked alternative.** Treating the original sensor result as settled O and exporting `open(V12)=true` downstream would be weak-E-to-O laundering. Treating the provenance downgrade as merely advisory while keeping prior closure active would be replay-skipping.

**Downstream projection.** Before replay, (\Pi_B) is not ready. After replay and revision, (\Pi_B(open(V12))=0). The D gate on remote actuation remains closed during the residualized interval.

### Example 2 — empirical claim from O/E tension to bounded verdict

**Initial mixed state.** The claim is “Drug A reduces blood pressure in adults with condition X.” O contains a candidate causal relation. E contains one observational study, one RCT with smaller effect, and unresolved subgroup heterogeneity. U pressures toward wide recommendation because the drug is cheap and available. D has no active guideline verdict yet.

**Lawful path.** The causal candidate becomes an E-claim. Evidence accumulates. Counterfactual branching splits by subgroup. External-validity claims are weakened because transport is unresolved. The universal (E\to O) move to “Drug A works for all adults with X” is blocked. The lawful move is a narrower (E\to O) commitment only for the evidenced subgroup and dose regime. The unresolved generality question remains residualized rather than forced closed. The utility surface is updated downward for wide rollout because expected value under uncertainty worsens. Repeated successful resolution of similar cases may later be harvested into a narrowing template such as “branch by subgroup before broad O-commitment,” but that template remains a prior, not an authority source.  

**Blocked alternative.** U cannot lawfully convert a narrow evidentially supported result into a broad ontic commitment. Nor can a counterfactual branch result be imported into actuality without fresh actual-world evidence.

**Downstream projection.** A bounded (\Pi_P) over the supported subgroup is ready. A universal Boolean efficacy verdict is not.

### Example 3 — governance/compliance case where E grounds D

**Initial mixed state.** Incident (I): a customer data store is exposed. O contains incident identity, dataset identity, and temporal facts. E contains logs, asset inventory, classification register, and DLP alerts. D contains regulation (n): if a personal-data breach is likely to create risk, notify within 72 hours. U wants to reduce harm and avoid unnecessary cost.

**Lawful path.** (O\to E) forms claims `personal_data_involved(I)` and `risk_likely(I)`. Evidence accumulates and lawfully grounds those antecedents. (E\to D) then binds an **existing** norm; it does not create a new one. If the norm’s fulfillment requires an occupied notification office, the D bundle must also carry an active office allocation witness for that office or escalate to the designated DPO/breach-response office. If such occupancy is present, `notify_within_72h(I)` becomes active and D constrains U so that optimization happens only over compliant response strategies. If occupancy is missing, the correct output is not “forbidden” and not “optional”; it is `escalate` to the appropriate office.  

**Blocked alternative.** U cannot mint a delay exemption because legal review is inconvenient. E cannot create a new notification rule from empirical seriousness alone. A public consumer of the rulebook cannot count as the notification office without an allocation witness.

**Downstream projection.** Once office closure and antecedent closure both hold, (\Pi_D(I)=\mathrm{required}(\text{notify_within_72h})). If the regulation is later compatibly superseded, future cases become `superseded`/`needs_replay`; historical compliance for the already settled case is not silently rewritten.

### Example 4 — strategy under D-bounds and E uncertainty

**Initial mixed state.** A drone must deliver temperature-sensitive medical supplies. O contains candidate routes (r_1,r_2,r_3). E contains weather forecasts, battery model, and wind-corridor uncertainty. D contains no-fly zones, risk thresholds, and cold-chain requirements. U wants minimum delivery time with maximum success rate.

**Lawful path.** (U\to O) constructs route candidates only as targets. (O\to U) evaluates them. (E\to U) updates values under forecast uncertainty. (O\to D) instantiates which rules each route triggers. (D\to U) filters the strategy space down to (A_{\mathrm{adm}}). Then (U)-optimization runs only over the admissible frontier. If an emergency waiver is contemplated, that appears first as (U\to D) petition and then requires a real exception office and witness path before the route can re-enter the admissible set.  

**Blocked alternative.** The globally shortest route (r_1) cannot stay in the feasible set merely because it is utility-attractive. Nor may a waiver be assumed without an occupied office carrying waiver rights.

**Downstream projection.** (\Pi_U) returns the best route among (A_{\mathrm{adm}}), not the shortest route simpliciter.

### Example 5 — exception/escalation case with office typing and replay

**Initial mixed state.** A critical production outage is live. O contains `SEV1(active)`. E contains telemetry, blast radius estimate, rollback readiness, and incomplete root-cause knowledge. D contains a normal rule requiring two approvals and tests before deploy, plus a break-glass exception requiring `SEV1`, rollback readiness, and incident-commander approval. U wants immediate service restoration.

**Lawful path.** O/E establish whether `SEV1` and rollback readiness are true. U may petition for break-glass. But (\mathrm{exc}_D) can bind only if a real office occupant holds the incident-commander role or a delegated subset of its exception rights with a valid delegation witness. If the shift lead merely “acts like” incident commander without office allocation, the case is blocked or escalated. If the delegation witness is valid, the exception binds locally for this incident and time window, not as a new standing norm. If later the break-glass policy is superseded, similar future cases become replay-sensitive: the old trace may be harvested as a template, but the next incident still needs current severity, rollback, office, and policy witnesses.   

**Blocked alternative.** “It is urgent, therefore permitted” is illicit U-to-D laundering. “The shift lead is effectively the commander because everyone agrees” is office-laundering absent allocation or delegation witness.

**Downstream projection.** Before valid office closure, (\Pi_D=\mathrm{escalate}) or `blocked`. After valid exception binding, (\Pi_D=\mathrm{permitted_under_exception}) for this scope only.

## 13. Final doctrine summary

1. **The retained hyperspace core survives intact.** ODEU is still best modeled as a witness-guarded stratified fibered state-space with partial typed morphisms. The hardening does not replace that object; it places it more exactly inside the membrane architecture.  

2. **The exact membrane fit is now clear.** (\mathcal X) is the state space of tracked coupled bundle states at checkpoints. A checkpoint is a finite tracked set plus residual, rejection, escalation, and reproposal registers. A settlement is a recomposed output over a checkpoint. ODEU is therefore the internal semantic geometry of the membrane, not the whole membrane transducer and not the downstream calculi. 

3. **The 2D cross remains valid but partial.** It is still the right first chart for O↔E and D↔U dominance. It remains only a chart because it cannot show office typing, witness-chain status, replay obligations, escalation routing, or residual/reproposal structure. 

4. **D requires office typing.** Binding force is not real enough if “authority” is only rhetorical. Office allocation, scope, delegation, nondelegable rights, escalation targets, override rights, succession, and office precedence are what make exception, override, revocation, and supersession semantically exact rather than verbal. Public access to law never implies office occupancy. 

5. **Witness law is chain law, not just bundle law.** The visible bundle at a transition is only the frontier of a larger dependency chain. Legality depends on chain coverage, active authority basis, effective interval, and material-premise continuity. Two surface-identical transitions can differ in legality because their witness chains differ.  

6. **Replay and supersession are part of the semantics.** Once a state’s admissibility depends on witnesses, offices, and derivations, upstream change must propagate. `stale`, `superseded`, `needs_replay`, and `invalid` are not implementation afterthoughts. They are semantic status conditions governing whether a state may continue to function as a lawful basis. 

7. **Residualization, reproposal, and harvest are lawful membrane outcomes.** Blocked or under-resolved material is not collapsed to false. It is routed into residual registers, structured reproposal demands, escalation queues, and later harvested playbooks or priors. Harvest improves future compilation but does not mint authority. 

8. **The anti-laundering doctrine remains hard and gets stronger.** U does not mint D. Weak E does not masquerade as settled O. Target construction is not actuality. Exception is not standing norm. A stale chain is not a live authorization. A harvested trace is not authority.  

9. **Machine-checkable blocks are now justified.** The hardening makes the doctrine explicit enough to expose structured state, morphism, witness-chain, readiness, laundering, and replay surfaces. That is why schema-like blocks at the end are appropriate. They are candidate support surfaces shaped for structured-input and warning-level use, not self-promoting APIs.  

10. **This note remains support-layer doctrine, not already promoted family law.** Its strongest role is now clear: it is a hardened constitutional-doctrine candidate that fits the membrane architecture, vertical authority exactness, and repo-native schema posture closely enough to enter later support-admission and coherence-kernel paths without pretending it already governs implementation.  

The constitutional sentence therefore tightens to:

> ODEU is the internal witness-bearing geometry of tracked coupled bundle states inside the semantic membrane; its law is the law of admissible morphisms, office-real force, and replay-aware witness chains, all before bounded downstream adjudication.
