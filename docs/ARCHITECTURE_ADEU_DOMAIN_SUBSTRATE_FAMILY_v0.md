## 1. Direct Answer

ADEU needs one general domain-substrate family because externally grounded structural
law does not become governed digital law merely by being stored in generic digital
carriers.

The missing family must therefore define:

- how a source-domain structural law becomes a lawful digital substrate family;
- what a descendant domain family may inherit unchanged;
- what a descendant domain family may specialize lawfully;
- what a descendant may constrain but may not mint; and
- what machine-visible artifact ladder is required before descendant claims become
  more than support prose.

## 2. Position In The Stack

The stack should be read as:

1. repo-wide constitutional authoring law
2. recursive coordinate placement law
3. vertical authority/publicity descent law
4. ODEU local adjudication membrane law
5. general domain-substrate family
6. concrete descendant domain family
7. family-local instances and bounded operational artifacts

This family therefore does not replace the higher layers.

It particularizes them into one reusable form for domains where:

- source objects have real structural law;
- normalization or projection can launder authority if left implicit;
- evidence, admissibility, replay, and derived-feature barriers matter materially; and
- generic storage grammar alone is not constitutionally adequate.

## 3. What This Family Owns

The general domain-substrate family should own the reusable middle-layer law for:

- descendant family constitution shape;
- descendant inheritance and specialization boundaries;
- binding, admissibility, and projection posture;
- instance-manifest posture;
- normalization and derived-feature barrier posture;
- evidence, benchmark, and replay-adjacent support surfaces; and
- the machine-checkable claims that a thin coherence kernel may read.

It should not own:

- one concrete descendant domain by itself;
- runtime product behavior;
- broad autonomous reasoning over prose; or
- automatic promotion of support doctrine into release law.

## 4. General Artifact Ladder

The family-level ladder should be read as:

1. family architecture / decomposition artifact
2. family kernel-spec artifact
3. descendant family constitution
4. descendant family-local schema or contract artifacts
5. descendant instance or manifest artifacts
6. bounded normalization / projection / admissibility artifacts
7. evidence / benchmark / trial artifacts

Only some of these are selected in `V55-A`.

The first slice should instantiate the family architecture, kernel spec, and thin
checker/report surfaces only. Descendant constitutions and family-local contracts remain
later surfaces unless explicitly selected.

## 5. Descendant Entitlement Law

A lawful descendant domain family may inherit:

- recursive coordinate envelope and placement law;
- authority-layer declarations and lawful descent posture;
- membrane-boundary law and anti-laundering posture;
- general domain-substrate artifact roles; and
- checker-visible structured claim requirements.

A lawful descendant domain family may specialize:

- domain-local object families;
- domain-local admissibility rules;
- domain-local normalization and compaction law;
- domain-local carrier profiles; and
- domain-local benchmark or evidence surfaces.

A lawful descendant domain family may constrain but not mint:

- carrier-profile choices under inherited constitutional law;
- derived-feature visibility under inherited deontic barriers;
- projection surfaces under inherited authority boundaries; and
- family-local operator posture where parent authority already permits such narrowing.

A lawful descendant domain family may not mint:

- higher-layer authority for itself;
- exemption from recursive placement law;
- exemption from promotion or witness requirements;
- release-gating law by support citation alone; or
- runtime/product authority beyond what its parent law already permits.

## 6. Support-Doc Admission Law

Support artifacts may shape this family only through explicit admission.

Admission requires that each shaping support artifact declare:

- authority layer;
- current authority relation;
- allowed use inside the family or kernel;
- forbidden inference; and
- later promotion requirement.

So support artifacts may become:

- admitted shaping input;
- bounded trial input; or
- deferred support lineage.

They do not become:

- family law;
- checker law; or
- release-gating law

by citation alone.

## 7. Preferred First Descendant

The preferred first descendant trial is the bounded market-data descendant surface now
recorded in:

- `docs/support/crypto data spec2.md`

That preference exists because it already exercises, in one bounded support surface:

- descendant inheritance;
- binding law;
- admissibility law;
- projection barriers; and
- force monotonicity over derived features.

The trial remains support-surface mode only until a later lane selects stronger
authority.

## 8. Non-Goals

This family draft does not select:

- a general theorem prover;
- free-form prose interpretation as a checker default;
- immediate descendant runtime implementation;
- multi-descendant rollout in the starter lane; or
- repo-wide CI gating.
