## Gap

The repo still lacks a released way to:

- represent benchmarks as typed semantic measurement systems rather than as ad hoc task
  plus score bundles;
- distinguish general benchmark doctrine from concrete benchmark projections without
  collapsing the top-layer artifact family into the first benchmark family selected;
- capture subject-under-test identity and execution context cleanly across:
  - base models;
  - prompted models;
  - routed runtimes;
  - multi-agent systems;
  - ADEU harness surfaces;
- validate the benchmark itself for stability, noise, trace adequacy, and
  reproducibility rather than only score the subject under test;
- carry explicit reliability semantics for:
  - stable baseline;
  - reliable `100%`;
  - acceptable variance;
  - non-regression floors;
- keep benchmark diagnostics explicitly non-promotional unless a separate downstream
  promotion law exists.

The missing layer is therefore not merely another benchmark idea and not yet a routing,
role-assignment, or training-governance doctrine.

The missing layer is an ADEU benchmark substrate.

## Relationship To `V43`, `V44`, And `V45`

`V43`, `V44`, `V45`, and this new candidate family are connected but non-identical.

`V43` asks:

- what kind of external contest world is this?
- what is lawful?
- what is measured?
- where could reasoning AI help?

`V44` asks and now answers on `main`:

- is a candidate model structurally suitable to inhabit an explicit inferential
  skeleton under ADEU discipline?

`V45` asks:

- what does the ADEU repo currently contain, and how are those surfaces typed and
  governed as a self-described artifact system?

This new family asks:

- how should ADEU Studio define benchmark families, benchmark projections, execution
  contexts, validation reports, traces, and diagnostic interpretation as reusable
  governed objects across many possible subjects under test?

So this family may constrain:

- later evaluation surfaces used by contest or runtime branches;
- how structural-assessment ideas become concrete benchmark projections;
- how ADEU harness or repo surfaces may be benchmarked as subjects under test;
- what benchmark outputs are epistemically justified before downstream consumers act on
  them.

But it may not mint:

- contest law;
- structural-assessment doctrine;
- repo self-description doctrine;
- routing authority;
- role-assignment authority;
- training or distillation entitlement.

Planning relationship:

- `V43` remains a valid connected candidate family from `v26`;
- `V44` remains a connected released family that may now constrain `V46`;
- `V45` remains a valid connected candidate family from `v28`;
- this draft introduces a fourth connected candidate family rather than replacing any of
  them;
- whether `V46` should precede, run in parallel with, or later constrain those other
  branches remains planning-bound and not yet locked.

## Closed Earlier Families And Surfaces

`V41` should now be read as a released practical-analysis substrate that already proved
ADEU can analyze bounded artifact worlds through explicit lane discipline.

`V42` should now be read as a released external challenge specialization that proved
ADEU can run a bounded reasoning/evidence control plane over one concrete challenge
family.

Neither `V41` nor `V42` released a general benchmark substrate that:

- separates family-level benchmark doctrine from projection-level benchmark doctrine;
- captures subject-under-test identity and execution context explicitly;
- validates the benchmark family itself as an instrument;
- keeps benchmark diagnostics explicitly non-promotional by default.

`V46` is the planning move that generalizes the benchmarking thread without reopening
practical-analysis, contest, structural-assessment, or repo-self-description planning
branches.

## Recommended Next Family (`V46`)

`V46` should release a bounded benchmark doctrine around:

- benchmark family specs;
- benchmark projection specs;
- benchmark instance specs;
- benchmark execution contexts;
- benchmark run traces;
- benchmark metrics and diagnostic reports;
- benchmark validation reports over the benchmark instrument itself;
- explicit subject-under-test taxonomy;
- explicit reliability semantics;
- explicit diagnostic-to-promotion boundaries;
- hierarchical execution traces when the projection carries parent/child action
  structure;
- explicit diagnostic separation between:
  - horizontal plan-spine fidelity,
  - vertical active-step compilation fidelity,
  - reintegration fidelity.

The family should treat concrete benchmark projections such as Procedural Depth Fidelity
as:

- downstream consumers of the general benchmark substrate;
- not the first artifact family selected in isolation from that substrate.

The family should treat outputs such as routing, role-fit, or training proposals as:

- possible later consumers of benchmark diagnostics;
- not authorities minted by the benchmark substrate itself.

The family should treat benchmark validity as first-class.

The starter `V46-A` taxonomy is intentionally narrower than the broader benchmarking
seed:

- composite `human_plus_tool_plus_model` subject-under-test posture remains deferred
  from the starter vocabulary;
- starter reliability and non-regression summaries remain declared substrate policy
  only until later projection lanes release empirical benchmark artifacts.

At minimum, later `V46` work should make explicit:

- how repeated-run stability is measured;
- what counts as reliable `100%`;
- what variance floors invalidate stronger perturbation claims;
- how benchmark output postures carry:
  - observed;
  - derived deterministically;
  - inferred interpretively;
  - adjudicated;
  - settled;
- how benchmark validation reports bind to:
  - reproducibility;
  - scorer determinism;
  - gold adequacy;
  - baseline noise floor.

## Recommended Next Path (`V46-C`)

Implement the first Procedural Depth perturbation and non-regression widening next.

`V46-C` should introduce:

- one bounded perturbation-case family over the released `V46-B` baseline instance and
  gold-trace world;
- one bounded perturbation-run evaluation lane that reuses the released
  `adeu_procedural_depth_run_trace@1`, `adeu_procedural_depth_metrics@1`, and
  `adeu_procedural_depth_diagnostic_report@1` contracts rather than forking them;
- one bounded failure-topology summary over repeated perturbation cases;
- one bounded non-regression report over repeated perturbation replays;
- one bounded benchmark-validation report for the perturbation bundle itself;
- explicit reuse of the released `V46-A` family/projection/execution-context substrate
  and the released `V46-B` baseline procedural-depth stack;
- explicit repeated-run stability and non-regression posture without widening yet into
  cross-subject comparison or downstream operational promotion.

`V46-C` is still diagnostic-first and non-promotional:

- it may widen procedural-depth evaluation into perturbation and non-regression law;
- it may not yet emit cross-subject ranking surfaces, projection-library sprawl, or
  routing/role/training authority;
- it may not yet widen into the benchmark-library and comparison seams that belong to
  later `V46-D` and `V46-E` paths.

## Why This Path

- It is the narrowest safe next consumer of the now-released procedural-depth baseline.
- It preserves the ladder already selected by the family:
  - `V46-A` substrate first
  - `V46-B` first concrete projection second
  - `V46-C` perturbation and non-regression only after a released baseline exists
- It makes repeated-run stability and non-regression explicit before the family widens
  into broader benchmark-library or cross-subject comparison claims.
- It keeps the family from collapsing prematurely into stealth leaderboard logic,
  cross-subject ranking, or operational promotion from benchmark diagnostics.
