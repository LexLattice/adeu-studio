# Draft Next Arc Options v29

Status: planning draft after `docs/DRAFT_NEXT_ARC_OPTIONS_v28.md`, updated after the
benchmarking-module seed and its first procedural-depth projection were clarified as a
separate but connected family direction, and refreshed after the full internal `V44`
and `V46` families closed on `main`.

This draft does not automatically supersede the contest-participation planning branch in
`docs/DRAFT_NEXT_ARC_OPTIONS_v26.md`, the structural-reasoning assessment planning
branch in `docs/DRAFT_NEXT_ARC_OPTIONS_v27.md`, or the repo self-description planning
branch in `docs/DRAFT_NEXT_ARC_OPTIONS_v28.md`.

Instead, it records a fourth connected candidate family:

how should ADEU Studio represent, run, validate, and interpret benchmarks as governed
semantic measurement systems before widening into projection sprawl, implicit
leaderboard doctrine, or downstream operational promotion from diagnostic outputs?

This is a planning document only. It is not a lock doc and does not authorize runtime
behavior, schema release, or implementation by itself.

## Relationship To `V43`, `V44`, And `V45`

`V43`, `V44`, `V45`, and this new candidate family are connected but non-identical.

`V43` asks:

- what kind of external contest world is this?
- what is lawful?
- what is measured?
- where could reasoning AI help?

`V44` asks and now answers on `main`:

- is a candidate model structurally suitable to inhabit an explicit inferential
  skeleton under ADEU discipline?

`V45` asks:

- what does the ADEU repo currently contain, and how are those surfaces typed and
  governed as a self-described artifact system?

This new family asks:

- how should ADEU Studio define benchmark families, benchmark projections, execution
  contexts, validation reports, traces, and diagnostic interpretation as reusable
  governed objects across many possible subjects under test?

So this family may constrain:

- later evaluation surfaces used by contest or runtime branches;
- how structural-assessment ideas become concrete benchmark projections;
- how ADEU harness or repo surfaces may be benchmarked as subjects under test;
- what benchmark outputs are epistemically justified before downstream consumers act on
  them.

But it may not mint:

- contest law;
- structural-assessment doctrine;
- repo self-description doctrine;
- routing authority;
- role-assignment authority;
- training or distillation entitlement.

Planning relationship:

- `V43` remains a valid connected candidate family from `v26`;
- `V44` remains a connected released family that may now constrain `V46`;
- `V45` remains a valid connected candidate family from `v28`;
- this draft introduces a fourth connected candidate family rather than replacing any of
  them;
- whether `V46` should precede, run in parallel with, or later constrain those other
  branches remains planning-bound and not yet locked.

## Recommended Next Family (`V46`)

`V46` should release a bounded benchmark doctrine around:

- benchmark family specs;
- benchmark projection specs;
- benchmark instance specs;
- benchmark execution contexts;
- benchmark run traces;
- benchmark metrics and diagnostic reports;
- benchmark validation reports over the benchmark instrument itself;
- explicit subject-under-test taxonomy;
- explicit reliability semantics;
- explicit diagnostic-to-promotion boundaries;
- hierarchical execution traces when the projection carries parent/child action
  structure;
- explicit diagnostic separation between:
  - horizontal plan-spine fidelity,
  - vertical active-step compilation fidelity,
  - reintegration fidelity.

The family should treat concrete benchmark projections such as Procedural Depth Fidelity
as:

- downstream consumers of the general benchmark substrate;
- not the first artifact family selected in isolation from that substrate.

The family should treat outputs such as routing, role-fit, or training proposals as:

- possible later consumers of benchmark diagnostics;
- not authorities minted by the benchmark substrate itself.

The family should treat benchmark validity as first-class.

The starter `V46-A` taxonomy is intentionally narrower than the broader benchmarking
seed:

- composite `human_plus_tool_plus_model` subject-under-test posture remains deferred
  from the starter vocabulary;
- starter reliability and non-regression summaries remain declared substrate policy
  only until later projection lanes release empirical benchmark artifacts.

At minimum, later `V46` work should make explicit:

- how repeated-run stability is measured;
- what counts as reliable `100%`;
- what variance floors invalidate stronger perturbation claims;
- how benchmark output postures carry:
  - observed;
  - derived deterministically;
  - inferred interpretively;
  - adjudicated;
  - settled;
- how benchmark validation reports bind to:
  - reproducibility;
  - scorer determinism;
  - gold adequacy;
  - baseline noise floor.
