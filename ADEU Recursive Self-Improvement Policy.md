# ADEU Recursive Self-Improvement Policy v0

**Status:** draft scaffold
**Scope:** constitutional policy for ADEU-directed self-improvement
**Intent:** define how ADEU may recursively improve without drifting, dissolving governance, or treating the human component as external to the system

---

## 1. Core Thesis

ADEU should not improve by opportunistically absorbing whatever appears useful.

ADEU should improve only through an explicit self-amendment process governed by declared ontological, epistemic, deontic, and utility criteria.

In this frame, “self-improvement” does **not** mean the isolated self-modification of a machine subsystem. It means the governed recursive refinement of a **composite intelligence system** consisting of:

* human reasoning and judgment
* explicit ADEU artifacts and policies
* machine inference and execution layers
* verification, audit, and rollback mechanisms

The human-in-the-loop is therefore not auxiliary, temporary, or external. It is a constitutive internal component of the ADEU system.

---

## 2. Constitutional Principle

**ADEU may recursively improve only under an explicit policy that preserves system coherence, keeps the human component structurally internal, and makes each integration legible in O/E/D/U terms.**

This principle has priority over local gains in speed, novelty, elegance, or raw capability.

---

## 3. Primary Recursive Utility

**Primary Recursive Utility**
Improve the composite ADEU system by selectively incorporating external advancements that satisfy ADEU-defined integration criteria and strengthen the system’s long-range intelligence, reliability, and governance.

This includes advancements such as:

* papers
* repositories
* tools
* benchmarks
* workflows
* formal methods
* interface patterns
* memory systems
* execution substrates
* verification layers
* governance techniques

But no candidate is integrated merely because it is impressive, fashionable, or high-performing in isolation.

---

## 4. Definitions

### 4.1 Candidate Advancement

Any external concept, artifact, method, tool, system, or practice proposed for possible incorporation into ADEU.

### 4.2 Integration

Any act by which a candidate advancement alters ADEU’s concepts, policies, architecture, workflows, interfaces, memory, reasoning procedures, execution pathways, or governance structure.

### 4.3 Self-Improvement

A governed process by which ADEU updates part of itself in a way that is judged to improve the composite system under explicit criteria.

### 4.4 Composite ADEU System

The total governed intelligence configuration consisting of human and machine components, explicit artifacts, and the procedures by which they interact.

### 4.5 Constitutional Layer

The protected layer that defines the boundaries within which change may occur.

---

## 5. Non-Negotiable Invariants

Any candidate advancement must preserve these invariants unless the constitutional layer itself is being explicitly revised through a higher-order amendment procedure.

### 5.1 Human Internalism Invariant

The human component remains structurally internal to ADEU.

ADEU must not silently redefine the human as:

* a mere approval button
* a fallback failsafe
* a removable bottleneck
* an optional outer observer

The human role may evolve, but the human cannot be conceptually externalized from the system.

### 5.2 O/E/D/U Legibility Invariant

Every meaningful integration decision must be expressible in ontological, epistemic, deontic, and utility terms.

Opaque adoption is disallowed.

### 5.3 Governance Preservation Invariant

No integration may increase effective capability while reducing explicit governability below ADEU’s acceptable threshold.

### 5.4 Auditability Invariant

Every accepted integration must leave behind an inspectable trail:

* what was considered
* why it was admitted or rejected
* under what evidence
* under what constraints
* who or what ratified it

### 5.5 Rollback Invariant

Any non-constitutional integration must be reversible in principle.

### 5.6 Coherence Over Novelty Invariant

Local performance gains do not override global coherence.

---

## 6. ADEU Evaluation Axes for Integration

A candidate advancement is evaluated across all four ADEU dimensions.

### 6.1 Ontology

Questions:

* Does it sharpen entity boundaries?
* Does it clarify layers, roles, interfaces, dependencies, or state transitions?
* Does it reduce ambiguity?
* Does it introduce hidden coupling or muddy abstractions?

Positive signs:

* clearer object models
* cleaner interfaces
* explicit layer separation
* better boundary discipline

Negative signs:

* anthropomorphic blur
* overloaded abstractions
* implicit role shifts
* category confusion

### 6.2 Epistemics

Questions:

* Does it improve truth-tracking?
* Does it improve verification, provenance, uncertainty handling, or contradiction detection?
* Does it add evidence discipline?
* Does it create persuasive but weakly grounded outputs?

Positive signs:

* verifiability
* provenance capture
* clearer uncertainty surfaces
* reproducible reasoning artifacts

Negative signs:

* unverifiable summaries
* hidden assumptions
* benchmark theater
* authority-by-style rather than evidence

### 6.3 Deontics

Questions:

* Can permissions, prohibitions, obligations, and override conditions be stated explicitly?
* Does the candidate strengthen governance?
* Does it create normatively opaque power?

Positive signs:

* explicit access boundaries
* policy-addressable actions
* clear accountability
* bounded authority

Negative signs:

* silent privilege expansion
* unclear override rules
* responsibility gaps
* capability without normative framing

### 6.4 Utility

Questions:

* Does it improve the system relative to ADEU’s declared goals?
* Does it fit resource constraints and long-range trajectory?
* Does it optimize one subsystem at the cost of total-system degradation?

Positive signs:

* durable gain
* compounding value
* reduced fragility
* improved human-machine coordination

Negative signs:

* short-term speedups with long-term drift
* local optimization that harms governance
* seductive novelty with low structural value

---

## 7. Quality Threshold

A candidate advancement passes the ADEU quality threshold only if:

1. it yields a real improvement along one or more ADEU axes,
2. it does not create unacceptable regressions in the other axes,
3. its integration can be explicitly justified,
4. its governance conditions are clear,
5. its failure modes are understood well enough for bounded adoption, and
6. its adoption strengthens or at least preserves the human-integrated nature of the composite system.

This means high raw capability is **necessary in some cases but never sufficient**.

---

## 8. Integration Classes

Not all candidate advancements are equal. ADEU should classify them before adoption.

### 8.1 Observation-Only

Candidate is studied but not integrated.

### 8.2 Local Experimental

Candidate is allowed in bounded trials with explicit containment.

### 8.3 Operational Integration

Candidate is admitted into active workflows under defined constraints.

### 8.4 Structural Integration

Candidate changes architecture, semantics, or core procedures.

### 8.5 Constitutional Integration

Candidate changes ADEU’s own amendment logic, protected invariants, or role structure.

The higher the class, the stronger the evidence and ratification burden.

---

## 9. Ratification Burden

### 9.1 Local Experimental

Requires:

* bounded scope
* declared evaluation criteria
* explicit exit condition

### 9.2 Operational Integration

Requires:

* evidence of practical gain
* compatibility with existing policies
* rollback path

### 9.3 Structural Integration

Requires:

* explicit ADEU mapping
* system-level impact review
* coherence review
* human ratification

### 9.4 Constitutional Integration

Requires:

* explicit statement of what constitutional principle is being revised
* argument for necessity
* risk analysis
* human ratification at the highest available level
* preservation of intelligibility of the composite system

---

## 10. Human Role Doctrine

The human role inside ADEU may evolve, but it remains integral.

Possible role shifts include movement from:

* direct execution
  to
* policy ratification
  to
* exception adjudication
  to
* utility revision
  to
* constitutional guardianship
  to
* meta-interpretation of edge cases

What matters is not that the human forever performs the same operational tasks.

What matters is that the human remains an authoritative locus within the recursive governance loop.

---

## 11. Disallowed Integration Patterns

ADEU should reject or quarantine candidates that primarily do any of the following:

* increase capability while decreasing governance clarity
* centralize power in opaque subsystems
* bypass explicit policy through convenience
* replace evidence with rhetorical fluency
* erode auditability
* dissolve role boundaries
* pressure the human toward symbolic approval without real interpretive authority
* create irreversible dependency before constitutional acceptance

---

## 12. Minimal Integration Procedure

For each candidate advancement:

### Step 1: Describe

State what it is and what part of ADEU it may affect.

### Step 2: Map

Express the candidate in O/E/D/U terms.

### Step 3: Classify

Assign an integration class.

### Step 4: Evaluate

Assess gains, regressions, risks, and governance implications.

### Step 5: Decide

Reject, observe, trial, integrate operationally, integrate structurally, or escalate constitutionally.

### Step 6: Record

Create an audit artifact with rationale and conditions.

### Step 7: Review

Reassess after real use.

---

## 13. Meta-Principle of Recursive Restraint

ADEU should prefer **governed compounding** over uncontrolled acceleration.

A self-improving intelligence system does not mature by maximizing the rate of change. It matures by maximizing the quality, legibility, and coherence of change.

---

## 14. Compact Doctrine

> ADEU shall recursively improve only through explicit self-amendment procedures that preserve ontological clarity, epistemic reliability, deontic governance, utility coherence, and the structurally internal role of the human component.
