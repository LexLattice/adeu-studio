## 1. Framing and retained backbone

I will treat the retained backbone as constitutional.

The core consequence is this: ODEU is not four boxes and not four numbers. It is one coupled semantic object with four irreducible regimes of force:

* **O**: what is being posited as world-structure, state, objecthood, relation, or change.
* **E**: what warrants, challenges, suspends, or counterfactually stresses those positings.
* **D**: what binds, forbids, permits, gates, escalates, or overrides.
* **U**: what is sought, optimized, traded off, or strategically preferred under bounded conditions.

The familiar cross remains valid, but only as a first chart of a richer object. The center is not emptiness; it is live semantic material whose lane separation is still incomplete or still highly coupled. The quadrants are not isolated boxes; they are **dominance zones** inside a common field.

Most importantly, ODEU is the last semantic membrane before downstream bounded procedures. It does not replace Boolean closure, probability, rule engines, or optimization. It decides what may lawfully be handed to them.

## 2. Choice of formal object

The weakest adequate formal object is:

[
\mathfrak{X}
============

\bigl(
p:\mathcal{X}\to B,\ \mathrm{Strat},\ \mathcal{M},\ \mathbb{W},\ \mathbb{A},\ \Pi
\bigr)
]

where (\mathfrak{X}) is a **witness-guarded stratified fibered state-space with partial typed morphisms**.

### Why this is the right minimal object

A **plain typed graph** is too weak. It can encode support, attack, or authority edges, but it does not naturally capture:

* local mixed loading across O/E/D/U at one semantic locus,
* dominance zones and projections,
* path-sensitive transitions,
* different admissibility laws for different regions.

A **manifold** or “latent space” is too strong and too vague. It suggests smoothness, metrics, and continuous coordinates that we do not have warrant to assume.

A **pure sheaf-like object** is not enough. Sheaves are good for local-to-global consistency, but they do not by themselves encode guarded state change, authority gating, or defeasible operator law.

A **full category-theoretic treatment** is heavier than needed. We do need composition, but only of guarded partial morphisms with witness conditions. A partial transition doctrine is enough.

### What each component does

(B) is a base of **semantic loci**. A locus is a bounded semantic situation, such as a subject under a scope, time, jurisdiction, and task context.

(\mathcal{X}) is the total space of ODEU states. Over each (b\in B) there is a local fiber (\mathcal{X}_b) of admissible semantic packets at that locus.

(\mathrm{Strat}) stratifies (\mathcal{X}) into regions by lane dominance, conflict pattern, and blocking condition. This gives the “field with regimes” structure.

(\mathcal{M}) is the family of **partial typed morphisms**. They are not free arrows. They are guarded and witness-bearing.

(\mathbb{W}) is the witness system: evidence, provenance, authority, scope, derivation, exception, supersession, and counterfactual attachments.

(\mathbb{A}) is the provenance and authority structure: source quality orders, authority preorders, scope applicability, temporal validity, supersession precedence.

(\Pi) is the family of downstream projection operators. These are partial: only certain ODEU states are ready to cross the membrane.

### Fibered form

For each locus (b),

[
\mathcal{X}_b
\subseteq
O_b \times E_b \times D_b \times U_b \times \Lambda_b \times \Omega_b \times H_b
]

where (O_b,E_b,D_b,U_b) are lane states, (\Lambda_b) is the local coupling structure, (\Omega_b) is the witness bundle space, and (H_b) is lineage/history.

This is fibered because the same locus can carry nontrivial O, E, D, and U content simultaneously.

This is stratified because different packets occupy different dominance regimes.

This is transition-bearing because lawful movement is encoded by (\mathcal{M}), not inferred after the fact.

## 3. The 2D chart/projection and what it preserves

The pedagogical ODEU cross is a chart of the richer space, not the space itself.

Define lane observables

[
\rho_O,\rho_E,\rho_D,\rho_U : \mathcal{X}\to \mathbb{R}_{\ge 0}
]

where each (\rho_L(x)) is a **derived observable** measuring the active semantic loading of lane (L) in state (x). These are not the lane itself. They are summary functionals of the structured lane state.

Then define the 2D chart

[
\chi(x)
=======

\bigl(\chi_{OE}(x),\chi_{DU}(x)\bigr)
]

with

[
\chi_{OE}(x)
============

\frac{\rho_O(x)-\rho_E(x)}
{\rho_O(x)+\rho_E(x)+\varepsilon},
\qquad
\chi_{DU}(x)
============

\frac{\rho_D(x)-\rho_U(x)}
{\rho_D(x)+\rho_U(x)+\varepsilon}
]

for small (\varepsilon>0).

### What the chart preserves

It preserves first-order paired dominance:

* whether the state is more **ontically committed** or more **epistemically contested/suspended** in the O/E pair,
* whether it is more **deontically bound** or more **utility-driven/optimizing** in the D/U pair.

It preserves the fact that O/E and D/U are the privileged visible tensions.

It preserves quadrant structure as a sign pattern:

* ((+,+)): O-dominant and D-dominant,
* ((-,+)): E-dominant and D-dominant,
* ((+,-)): O-dominant and U-dominant,
* ((-,-)): E-dominant and U-dominant.

But these are not boxes. They are regions in which one side of each pair dominates.

### What the chart collapses

It necessarily forgets:

* the internal structure of O, E, D, U,
* the support/attack graph inside E,
* provenance quality,
* authority rank and scope,
* exception and supersession structure,
* the difference between insufficiency and contradiction unless encoded in the observables,
* path history,
* witness bundles,
* branch structure,
* the difference between lawful and laundered arrival at the same visible point.

### Why O ↔ E and D ↔ U are the visible axes

Because those are the two principal coupled tensions:

* **O ↔ E** is the tension between candidate world-commitment and witness-bearing contestability.
* **D ↔ U** is the tension between admissibility/binding and desirability/optimization.

E is not “negative O.” It is the regime in which claims are separated from their warrant, challenge, suspension, and counterfactual stress. The chart shows paired opposition, not additive negation.

### The center

The center of the chart is the region where pairwise dominance is weak. That does **not** mean the state is trivial.

A state can be central because:

* lane separation is incomplete,
* support and attack are both active,
* deontic applicability is unresolved,
* utility pressure exists but is not yet action-guiding,
* cross-lane coupling is high.

So the center is best understood as a live entanglement zone, not a null point.

## 4. Internal structure of O, E, D, U

### O: ontology / world-structure

A local O-state is minimally

[
o = (G_O,\ \Xi_O,\ T_O,\ P_O)
]

where (G_O) is a typed relational or state graph, (\Xi_O) is an incompatibility/exclusion relation, (T_O) is an ontic transition relation, and (P_O) is a persistence/change map.

This is enough to encode:

* candidate world-state,
* entities, relations, statehood, and structure,
* explicit absence as an ontic state,
* contradiction as incompatible assignment within the same ontic slot,
* persistence vs change through time or process.

Crucial distinction: in O, **absence** can be a world-state. In E, **unknown** is epistemic. They are not the same.

### E: epistemics / witness-bearing assessment

A local E-state is minimally

[
e = (C_E,\ S_E,\ A_E,\ P_E,\ K_E,\ B_E,\ \Sigma_E)
]

where:

* (C_E): claims, hypotheses, questions,
* (S_E): support relation,
* (A_E): attack/falsification relation,
* (P_E): provenance map and source-quality structure,
* (K_E): confidence geometry,
* (B_E): counterfactual branch structure,
* (\Sigma_E): epistemic posture.

The minimum useful confidence geometry is not a scalar but a tuple such as

[
K_E(c) = (\mathrm{sup},\ \mathrm{att},\ \mathrm{res})
]

for support, attack pressure, and unresolved residual.

That gives you the key distinction:

* **insufficiency**: high residual, not necessarily high attack,
* **contradiction/falsification pressure**: high attack,
* **supported but defeasible**: high support, low attack, nonzero residual.

This is the ADEU-native reason E is not just the “negative side” of O.

### D: deontics / binding force

A local D-state is minimally

[
d = (N_D,\ G_D,\ \preceq_D,\ Exc_D,\ Esc_D,\ Sup_D)
]

where:

* (N_D): norms,
* (G_D): gates,
* (\preceq_D): deontic precedence relation,
* (Exc_D): exception structure,
* (Esc_D): escalation structure,
* (Sup_D): revocation/supersession structure.

Each norm (n\in N_D) has at least:

[
n = (\mathrm{mod},\ \mathrm{cond},\ \mathrm{scope},\ \mathrm{target},\ \mathrm{auth},\ \mathrm{prio},\ \mathrm{state})
]

with modality (\mathrm{mod}\in{\mathrm{required},\mathrm{forbidden},\mathrm{permitted}}).

This is enough to encode:

* requirement, prohibition, permission,
* scope and applicability,
* explicit authority source,
* override and exception,
* revocation and supersession,
* escalation offices and triggers,
* fail-closed or fail-open behavior.

Permission is not mere absence of prohibition. It is its own explicit deontic object when present.

### U: utility / preference / strategy

A local U-state is minimally

[
u = (G_U^{loc},\ G_U^{glob},\ \preceq_U,\ V_U,\ \mathcal{F}_U,\ T_U)
]

where:

* (G_U^{loc}): local goals,
* (G_U^{glob}): global goals,
* (\preceq_U): priority or preference preorder,
* (V_U): value model or expected utility model,
* (\mathcal{F}_U): feasible strategy family before D-filtering,
* (T_U): explicit tradeoff rules and optimization posture.

This is enough to encode:

* preference,
* optimization pressure,
* local vs global objective,
* expected utility under uncertainty,
* tradeoffs,
* bounded strategy search,
* risk posture and stopping rules.

Utility is not deontics. A goal can strongly matter without becoming an obligation.

## 5. Formal representation of semantic states

A semantic state at locus (b) is a packet

[
x
=

\langle
b,\ o,\ e,\ d,\ u,\ \Lambda,\ \omega,\ h,\ \rho,\ \eta,\ \sigma
\rangle
]

where:

* (b): semantic locus,
* (o,e,d,u): lane states,
* (\Lambda): typed coupling graph,
* (\omega): active witness bundle,
* (h): lineage trace or dependency DAG,
* (\rho=(\rho_O,\rho_E,\rho_D,\rho_U)): lane observables,
* (\eta): entanglement/separability defect,
* (\sigma): status.

### Coupling graph

(\Lambda) is where the “coupled field” actually lives. Typical coupling types are:

* `about`: E-claim about O-object/state,
* `supports`, `refutes`: E-internal evidence relations,
* `justifies`: E to D grounding,
* `gates`: D gate requiring E or O conditions,
* `excepts`: D exception relation,
* `serves_goal`: O/E/D item connected to U goal,
* `prioritizes`: U priority relation,
* `instantiates`: O fact activates D applicability,
* `predicts`: O/E state feeds U outcome estimate.

### Status

(\sigma) takes values such as:

* `open`,
* `suspended`,
* `blocked`,
* `escalated`,
* `superseded`,
* `ready[Π_B]`,
* `ready[Π_P]`,
* `ready[Π_D]`,
* `ready[Π_U]`.

A state can be ready for one downstream operator and not for another.

### Mixed loading

A packet is mixed when several lane components are nontrivial at once. That is the normal case, not the edge case.

Local dominance is a derived fact about (\rho), not a replacement for the packet.

### A serializable shape

A future ADEU implementation could serialize a packet roughly as:

```json
{
  "locus": {"subject": "...", "scope": "...", "time": "...", "task": "..."},
  "O": {...},
  "E": {...},
  "D": {...},
  "U": {...},
  "couplings": [...],
  "witnesses": {...},
  "lineage": [...],
  "dominance": {"O": 0.61, "E": 0.77, "D": 0.20, "U": 0.54},
  "entanglement": 0.72,
  "status": ["suspended", "not_ready:Pi_D"]
}
```

Those numbers are chart observables only. The semantics remain in the structured lane payloads and witness bundles.

## 6. Morphism families

A morphism instance is written

[
x \xRightarrow[\alpha]{\mu:\omega} y
]

where (\mu) is a morphism family, (\omega) is the witness bundle presented for this transition, and (\alpha) is the authority context when relevant.

A morphism guard returns one of

[
G_\mu(x,\omega,\alpha)\in{\mathrm{admit},\mathrm{suspend},\mathrm{escalate},\mathrm{block}}
]

So a lawful transition system does not only produce successor states. It also lawfully produces suspension and escalation outcomes.

### O-internal morphisms

**(\mathrm{ref}_O): ontic refinement**
Source condition: same subject/locus, identity stable, no exclusion violated.
Change: add structure or narrow alternatives in (G_O).
Preserves: referential identity and prior satisfied structure as projection.
Witness: derivation witness; evidence witness if refinement claims new factual commitment.
Authority: none for local modeling; adjudicative authority if it updates an official record.
Mode: monotone in structural specificity; reversible only by lawful abstraction.
Failure: hidden replacement, alias collapse, scope widening.

**(\mathrm{rev}_O): ontic revision**
Source condition: existing O-state is defeated by contradiction, falsification, or better identity resolution.
Change: replace defeated subgraph with successor state.
Preserves: locus identity and defeated lineage.
Witness: revision witness plus defeating evidence lineage.
Authority: required for institutional “official state” updates.
Mode: nonmonotone and defeasible.
Failure: overwrite without tombstoning the defeated state.

**(\mathrm{excl}_O): contradiction/exclusion registration**
Source condition: two O-assignments contend for the same ontic slot or violate identity/exclusion law.
Change: mark co-instantiation as inadmissible.
Preserves: the competing alternatives themselves.
Witness: exclusion witness from ontology rules or evidence.
Authority: not usually needed.
Mode: blocking relation, reversible only by ontology repair or disambiguation.
Failure: confusing underdetermination with contradiction.

**(\mathrm{abs}_O) / (\mathrm{conc}_O): abstraction / concretization**
Abstraction removes detail while preserving a chosen entailment set.
Concretization adds detail either as hypothesis or evidence-backed specialization.
Witness: derivation witness for abstraction; evidence or explicit hypothetical tag for concretization.
Mode: abstraction is monotone in generality; concretization is defeasible.
Failure: smuggling speculative detail as settled world-state.

**(\mathrm{step}_O): ontic state transition**
Source condition: event, process, or temporal advancement applies.
Change: move from one world-state to another.
Preserves: continuity where identity is explicitly carried.
Witness: event, causal, procedural, or temporal witness.
Authority: needed if the transition is institutionally declared rather than merely modeled.
Mode: defeasible, partially reversible.
Failure: temporal jump with no transition witness.

### E-internal morphisms

**(\mathrm{acc}_E): evidence accumulation**
Change: add support edges, source items, or corroborating provenance.
Preserves: claim identity.
Witness: evidence and provenance witnesses.
Authority: none beyond source admissibility.
Mode: monotone in evidence set, not necessarily monotone in posture if new dependencies expose fragility.
Failure: duplicate counting, hidden dependence, provenance mismatch.

**(\mathrm{fals}_E): falsification / attack**
Change: add attack or refutation pressure against a claim.
Preserves: target claim identity and source lineage.
Witness: evidence witness plus bridge to claim content.
Mode: defeasible and often blocking.
Failure: treating mere absence as refutation.

**(\mathrm{weak}_E): weakening**
Change: lower support quality, increase residual, or downgrade posture.
Witness: provenance downgrade, method criticism, dependency collapse, or contradiction discovery.
Mode: nonmonotone and defeasible.
Failure: untracked downgrade of a claim’s warrant basis.

**(\mathrm{susp}_E): suspension**
Change: move a claim to open/underdetermined/suspended posture rather than yes/no.
Witness: insufficiency, unresolved conflict, or unresolved ambiguity.
Authority: none.
Mode: lawful nonclosure.
Failure: collapsing suspension into negation or acceptance.

**(\mathrm{prov}_E^{\uparrow/\downarrow}): provenance upgrade/downgrade**
Change: alter the source-quality order without changing claim content.
Witness: chain-of-custody repair, retraction, freshness update, method validation.
Mode: propagates to support quality and downstream readiness.
Failure: source prestige replacing actual provenance.

**(\mathrm{cf}_E): counterfactual branching**
Change: create branch states under altered assumptions.
Preserves: actual-world branch as distinct.
Witness: explicit counterfactual assumption bundle.
Mode: branching, not actualization.
Failure: importing branch results into actuality without bridge witness.

**(\mathrm{merge}_E): evidential merge / conflict registration**
Change: unify compatible evidence sets or register unresolved conflict.
Witness: entity resolution and provenance compatibility.
Mode: conditional; may end in merge or suspension.
Failure: false merge across incompatible subjects or scopes.

### D-internal morphisms

**(\mathrm{bind}_D): binding / applicability activation**
Source condition: a norm exists and its antecedent may apply at the current locus.
Change: mark a norm active for this case/scope.
Preserves: modality, source authority, and trace to the parent norm.
Witness: scope/applicability witness, usually with O/E trigger witnesses.
Authority: the authority of the existing norm must apply.
Mode: monotone while the norm remains in force.
Failure: binding outside scope or without trigger proof.

**(\mathrm{scope}_D^{\downarrow/\uparrow}): narrowing / broadening scope**
Narrowing restricts applicability; broadening enlarges it.
Witness: scope witness and promulgation witness.
Authority: same or higher authority than the norm. Broadening is stricter than narrowing.
Mode: narrowing often monotone in restriction; broadening is nonmonotone over the governed domain.
Failure: retrospective scope laundering.

**(\mathrm{rev}_D): revocation**
Change: deactivate a norm.
Witness: revocation witness with temporal and authority legitimacy.
Authority: same or higher than the source of force.
Mode: nonmonotone, propagates to descendants.
Failure: informal nonuse treated as revocation.

**(\mathrm{sup}_D): supersession**
Change: introduce a successor norm or gate that overtakes an older one.
Preserves: lineage to the superseded item.
Witness: supersession witness.
Authority: explicit precedence must hold.
Mode: nonmonotone and path-dependent.
Failure: same-level contradiction mislabeled as supersession.

**(\mathrm{exc}_D): exception handling**
Change: create a locally scoped carve-out from an otherwise active norm.
Preserves: the parent norm remains globally intact.
Witness: exception trigger, scope witness, and usually authority witness.
Authority: required unless the parent norm itself auto-authorizes the exception on fixed conditions.
Mode: local, defeasible, nonmonotone.
Failure: exception inflated into general permission.

**(\mathrm{esc}_D): escalation**
Change: route the case to a higher office or checkpoint.
Preserves: unresolved state rather than faking closure.
Witness: escalation trigger.
Authority: the escalation rule itself supplies the route.
Mode: blocking or suspension of lower-layer closure.
Failure: using escalation language without a real office or rule.

**(\mathrm{regr}_D): authority re-grounding**
Change: remap a norm’s source of force to a successor office or promulgator.
Witness: authority succession witness.
Authority: must itself be legitimate.
Mode: nontrivial; can preserve content while changing who may speak it.
Failure: cosmetic relabeling of authority.

### U-internal morphisms

**(\mathrm{repr}_U): reprioritization**
Change: reorder preferences or goals.
Preserves: goal identity.
Witness: explicit preference revision or declared context switch.
Authority: the legitimate utility source for that goal set.
Mode: reversible and defeasible.
Failure: silent utility drift.

**(\mathrm{rew}_U): utility reweighting**
Change: alter weights, risk penalties, or local/global balance.
Witness: declared value update, measured performance, or revised forecast.
Authority: actor or institution authorized to set the objective.
Mode: defeasible.
Failure: hidden objective rewrite.

**(\mathrm{ev}_U): expected-value update**
Change: revise (V_U) in light of new E-state or model changes.
Witness: derivation from evidence/model update.
Mode: not monotone in ranking.
Failure: treating evidential change as pure preference change.

**(\mathrm{opt}_U^B): bounded optimization**
Change: search within a bounded feasible set under current value model.
Preserves: declared objective and search budget.
Witness: derivation witness for the selected frontier or choice.
Authority: none beyond ownership of the goal set.
Mode: lawful only after D-filtering when D applies.
Failure: unconstrained optimization pretending D is optional.

**(\mathrm{branch}_U): strategy branching**
Change: generate alternative plans or target states.
Preserves: branch separation and assumptions.
Witness: scenario assumptions and search bounds.
Mode: branching, not closure.
Failure: candidate strategy treated as committed decision.

**(\mathrm{trade}_U): tradeoff transformation**
Change: rewrite the tradeoff rule itself, such as from lexicographic to weighted.
Witness: explicit tradeoff rule or authorized policy.
Authority: needed for collective or institutional objectives.
Mode: nonmonotone and path-sensitive.
Failure: post hoc tradeoff change to justify a favored result.

### Cross-lane morphisms

**(\mathrm{form}_{O\to E}): claim formation**
Source condition: there is an ontic candidate state or structure worth assessing.
Change: produce an assessable claim/hypothesis/question in E and couple it to O with `about`.
Preserves: subject identity and scope.
Witness: derivation witness mapping O-content to claim form.
Authority: none for local formulation.
Mode: monotone and reversible.
Failure: sending O directly downstream as if already warranted.

**(\mathrm{commit}_{E\to O}): ontic commitment / revision from evidence**
Source condition: an E-claim is sufficiently supported for the current commitment purpose, with no undefeated higher-rank challenge.
Change: instantiate or revise an O-state.
Preserves: subject mapping, scope, and defeated alternatives.
Witness: evidence, provenance, derivation, and sometimes institutional acceptance witness.
Authority: required if the result claims official rather than merely local ontic status.
Mode: defeasible and partially reversible.
Failure: weak E masquerading as settled O.

**(\mathrm{bind}_{E\to D}): evidential grounding of existing D**
Source condition: a D norm/gate already exists. E does not create it; E establishes that its condition applies.
Change: activate/bind the existing norm or gate for this case.
Preserves: parent norm identity and authority source.
Witness: evidence, provenance, scope, derivation.
Authority: inherited from the already-existing D item.
Mode: admissible when evidential trigger is enough.
Failure: empirical regularity treated as norm creation.

**(\mathrm{prop}_{E\to D}): norm proposal from evidence**
Source condition: evidence suggests a new or revised norm would be justified.
Change: produce a *proposed* deontic item, not an active one.
Witness: evidence and policy-formation lineage.
Authority: absent until an authorized office acts.
Mode: advisory only until promulgated.
Failure: proposal rendered as binding norm.

**(\mathrm{constr}_{D\to U}): deontic constraint on utility**
Source condition: applicable D items are active.
Change: shrink or shape the feasible utility search region.
Preserves: utility goals as goals; D does not rewrite what is desired, it limits what may be sought.
Witness: applicability closure of D.
Authority: inherited from D.
Mode: monotone in admissible-set restriction.
Failure: optimizing over forbidden actions.

**(\mathrm{pet}_{U\to D}): utility pressure / petition**
Source condition: utility pressure exists against the current D configuration.
Change: create a petition, exception request, or norm-change proposal.
Preserves: non-authoritative status.
Witness: utility rationale, often with E forecast.
Authority: none for the petition itself; authority only later if D changes.
Mode: advisory.
Failure: U directly minting D.

**(\mathrm{eval}_{O\to U}): ontic-to-utility evaluation**
Source condition: candidate states or actions are defined in O.
Change: feed outcome features into U’s value model.
Preserves: distinction between world-state and value assignment.
Witness: predictive/derivation witness.
Mode: lawful.
Failure: confusing “good” with “true”.

**(\mathrm{target}_{U\to O}): utility-seeking target construction**
Source condition: utility search generates desired states or plans.
Change: create candidate O targets or action plans.
Preserves: prospective status; these are not actual O facts.
Witness: strategy-generation witness.
Mode: lawful only as candidate generation.
Failure: desired future treated as present actuality.

**(\mathrm{upd}_{E\to U}): evidential update of value/risk**
Source condition: E changes expected outcomes, risks, or uncertainty.
Change: revise (V_U).
Preserves: utility objective identity.
Witness: evidence and derivation.
Mode: lawful and common.
Failure: evidence ignored because it is inconvenient.

**(\mathrm{inq}_{U\to E}): utility-guided inquiry**
Source condition: the system needs more evidence because it matters for goals.
Change: prioritize evidence gathering, measurement, or value-of-information branches.
Preserves: E’s autonomy over what counts as evidence.
Witness: utility rationale.
Mode: lawful only as inquiry selection, not as evidence fabrication.
Failure: desired outcome dictating evidential conclusion.

**(\mathrm{inst}_{O\to D}): ontic instantiation of D applicability/violation**
Source condition: a norm exists and O facts bear on whether it applies or is violated.
Change: instantiate compliance, noncompliance, or applicability relations.
Preserves: norm identity and authority source.
Witness: O/E trigger witnesses plus scope.
Mode: lawful when norm preexists.
Failure: fact pattern treated as norm creation.

**(\mathrm{env}_{D\to O}): deontic admissibility envelope over O**
Source condition: active norms constrain allowed states or transitions.
Change: annotate O-state or O-transition as admissible, inadmissible, or gated.
Preserves: distinction between actuality and permissibility.
Mode: lawful.
Failure: “permitted” confused with “actual,” or “forbidden” confused with “impossible.”

## 7. Witness-bearing transition law

The witness bundle is not decoration. It is part of the transition law.

Define

[
\omega
======

(
\omega^{ev},
\omega^{prov},
\omega^{auth},
\omega^{der},
\omega^{scope},
\omega^{sup},
\omega^{cf},
\omega^{exc}
)
]

where the components are:

* (\omega^{ev}): evidence witnesses,
* (\omega^{prov}): provenance witnesses,
* (\omega^{auth}): authority witnesses,
* (\omega^{der}): derivation witnesses,
* (\omega^{scope}): scope/applicability witnesses,
* (\omega^{sup}): supersession/revocation witnesses,
* (\omega^{cf}): counterfactual witnesses,
* (\omega^{exc}): exception/escalation witnesses.

A transition instance is lawful only if:

[
\mathrm{Lawful}*\mu(x,\omega,\alpha)
\iff
\mathrm{TypeOK}*\mu(\omega)
\land
\mathrm{ProvOK}(\omega)
\land
\mathrm{ScopeOK}(x,\omega)
\land
\mathrm{AuthOK}*\mu(\alpha,\omega)
\land
\mathrm{DeriveOK}*\mu(x,\omega)
\land
\mathrm{NoStrongerThanWitness}_\mu(x,\omega,\alpha)
]

### The core law: no stronger than witness

Let (\kappa(y\mid x)) be the commitment increase from source (x) to target (y), measured in the dimensions that matter here:

* scope,
* certainty,
* modality,
* authority.

Let (\mathrm{cov}(\omega,\alpha)) be the coverage granted by the witness bundle and authority context.

Then the transition is lawful only if

[
\kappa(y\mid x)\preceq \mathrm{cov}(\omega,\alpha)
]

This is the anti-laundering law.

A target may not be:

* broader in scope than its witnesses cover,
* more certain than its evidence justifies,
* stronger in modality than its authority allows,
* higher in authority than its source of force provides.

### Consequence

Two transitions may have the same visible source and target while differing in legitimacy because their witness bundles differ.

A `notify within 72h` outcome reached through statutory trigger + incident evidence is lawful. The same visible deontic outcome reached only because “it seems safer politically” is not.

## 8. Illicit laundering vs lawful transition

A visible state change is **semantic laundering** when a downstream surface behaves as though a lawful morphism occurred, but no admissible witness-bearing morphism actually licenses that move.

Formally, an apparent transition (x\leadsto y) is laundering if there exists no family (\mu), witness bundle (\omega), and authority context (\alpha) such that

[
x \xRightarrow[\alpha]{\mu:\omega} y
]

is lawful, yet (y) is treated as active, binding, or closed.

### Canonical laundering failures

**1. U directly minting D**
Invalid: “This is useful, therefore permitted/required.”
Lawful replacement: (U\to D) may only create a petition or proposal; a separate authority-bearing D act is needed for binding force.

**2. Weak E masquerading as settled O**
Invalid: “There is some evidence, therefore it is the case.”
Lawful replacement: E must either accumulate, weaken, suspend, or only then commit into O under an explicit threshold and witness profile.

**3. D retrofitted from convenience**
Invalid: “Because this would be efficient, the rule is now that we may do it.”
Lawful replacement: utility pressure can motivate rulemaking, but D changes require authorized promulgation, exception, or supersession.

**4. O passed downstream without E mediation**
Invalid: raw world-claims handed to Boolean closure or policy engines as if already warranted.
Lawful replacement: O must become assessable in E first, unless the downstream operator is explicitly taking an O-object as a mere candidate, not a fact.

**5. Exception claims inflated into standing norms**
Invalid: a local carve-out becomes a general permission.
Lawful replacement: exception stays local in scope, time, target, and authority.

**6. Override invoked without office**
Invalid: “Emergency” used as a free-floating normative solvent.
Lawful replacement: break-glass or escalation requires a real rule, a real trigger, and a real authority.

**7. U-generated target states treated as actual O-states**
Invalid: desired futures are narrated as present facts.
Lawful replacement: U may construct candidate O targets, but they remain prospective until realized and/or evidenced.

**8. Counterfactual branches imported into actuality**
Invalid: “Under assumption A, X would happen; therefore X is actually the case.”
Lawful replacement: branch results remain tagged by (\omega^{cf}) until re-bridged by actual evidence.

This doctrine can be stated compactly:

> No lane may export stronger semantic force than its own lawful witnesses and authority can carry.

## 9. Composition, reversibility, supersession, and path dependence

ODEU morphisms compose, but not freely.

### Partial composition

If

[
x \xRightarrow[\alpha_1]{\mu_1:\omega_1} y
\quad\text{and}\quad
y \xRightarrow[\alpha_2]{\mu_2:\omega_2} z
]

then (\mu_2\circ\mu_1) is admitted only if:

* target/source types match,
* scope and subject are compatible,
* no unresolved `suspend` or `escalate` outcome is being skipped,
* later witnesses do not silently invalidate earlier commitments unless the second morphism is explicitly a revision or supersession move.

So composition is **guarded partial composition**, not free category-style composition.

### Order matters

(E\to D) bind followed by (D\to U) constrain is lawful.

(U\to D) pressure followed by direct (D) activation is not lawful unless an authority-bearing D act intervenes.

(E) suspension followed by Boolean closure is not lawful unless the closure operator explicitly admits bounded uncertainty. Standard Boolean closure usually does not.

### Surface-equivalence vs lawful equivalence

Define (x \equiv_s y) when the visible lane contents match but lineage and witnesses are ignored.

Define (x \equiv_\ell y) when the visible lane contents match **and** the active witness/authority closure is equivalent.

ODEU requires (\equiv_\ell), not merely (\equiv_s), whenever legitimacy matters.

This is why path matters.

### Reversibility

Reversibility is never magic rollback. It is always witness-bearing.

* refinement can be partially reversed by abstraction,
* evidence accumulation can be weakened or retracted,
* ontic commitments can be revised under new evidence,
* bound norms can unbind by scope expiry or revocation,
* utility choices can be reconsidered when E or D changes.

Some reversals are exact; many are only partial because information, authority state, or historical force has changed.

### Supersession and revocation propagation

Let (\mathrm{dep}(x)) be the dependency set of witnesses, authorities, norms, and derivations on which state (x) depends.

If reference (r) is revoked or superseded, then every descendant state

[
\mathrm{Desc}(r)={x : r\in \mathrm{dep}(x)}
]

must be marked `superseded` or `needs_replay` until recomputed under the new source of force.

This is how path dependence becomes operational rather than rhetorical.

## 10. Membrane boundary to downstream adjudication

ODEU is the last semantic preprocessing membrane before bounded calculi.

There is **no global truth function**

[
v:\mathcal{X}\to{0,1}
]

and there is **no global utility function** over the whole ODEU object.

Instead there are projection-specific partial operators

[
\Pi_k : R_k \subseteq \mathcal{X} \to Y_k
]

where (R_k) is the readiness domain for projection (k).

A general readiness condition is:

[
R_k(x)
\iff
\mathrm{LaneSep}_k(x)
\land
\mathrm{WitnessClosed}_k(x)
\land
\mathrm{ScopeClosed}_k(x)
\land
\mathrm{AuthorityClosed}_k(x)
\land
\mathrm{AmbClosed}_k(x)
]

Here (\mathrm{AmbClosed}_k) means all high-impact ambiguities touching the footprint of (k) are either resolved or lawfully bracketed as bounded parameters.

### Boolean closure

[
\Pi_B : R_B \to {0,1}
]

Footprint: mainly O and E.

Prerequisites:

* claim tokenized,
* subject, time, and scope resolved,
* relevant support/attack posture closed enough for the chosen closure policy,
* no unresolved high-impact ambiguity on the claim.

What is lost at the boundary:

* the live support/attack graph,
* counterfactual branches,
* witness richness beyond retained refs,
* mixed lane loading.

### Probabilistic estimation

[
\Pi_P : R_P \to \text{intervals or distributions}
]

Footprint: O and E.

Prerequisites:

* hypotheses fixed,
* evidence model fixed,
* provenance quality acceptable,
* counterfactual assumptions explicitly bounded.

What is bracketed rather than eliminated:

* residual uncertainty may survive as interval width.

### Deontic verdict

[
\Pi_D : R_D \to
{\mathrm{required},\mathrm{forbidden},\mathrm{permitted},\mathrm{escalate},\mathrm{blocked}}
]

Footprint: O, E, D.

Prerequisites:

* norms and gates identified,
* scope/applicability closed,
* authority order fixed,
* exception/supersession status resolved.

What is lost:

* the broader D/U tension,
* the policy-formation history except via lineage refs.

### Utility selection

Let the deontically admissible action set be

[
A_{\mathrm{adm}}(x)={a\in \mathcal{F}_U(x) : a \text{ does not violate active } D\text{-constraints}}
]

Then a bounded utility selection is

[
\Pi_U(x)=\arg\max_{a\in A_{\mathrm{adm}}(x)} V_U(a\mid e_x)
]

or a Pareto frontier over (A_{\mathrm{adm}}(x)), under an explicit search budget.

Footprint: O, E, D, U.

Prerequisites:

* candidate strategy space fixed,
* D-filter applied,
* value model fixed,
* uncertainty model bounded.

### Membrane doctrine

Upstream in ODEU belong:

* claim formation,
* witness qualification,
* provenance binding,
* authority binding,
* applicability and scope analysis,
* exception and escalation analysis,
* candidate generation,
* ambiguity classification.

Downstream belong:

* Boolean closure,
* probability or interval estimation,
* deontic verdict engines,
* bounded optimization or action selection.

No downstream operator may mint fresh authority, invent missing evidence, or silently rewrite semantics. If it needs those, control returns upstream.

## 11. Worked examples

### Example 1 — factual claim under falsification pressure

**Initial mixed state**
A maintenance packet concerns valve (V12).

* (O): candidate state `open(V12)`.
* (E): one pressure sensor supports `open(V12)`; provenance is only moderate.
* (D): safety rule says remote actuation is gated if state is uncertain.
* (U): keep flow uninterrupted.

**Lawful path**

1. (\mathrm{form}_{O\to E}): create claim (c=)`open(V12)` with `about(c,V12)`.
2. (\mathrm{acc}_E): add sensor witness (w_s).
3. Camera image and manual inspection arrive. They support `closed(V12)`.
4. (\mathrm{fals}_E): attach attack against (c).
5. Calibration audit downgrades the sensor source. (\mathrm{prov}_E^\downarrow).
6. Because conflict is real and unresolved, (\mathrm{susp}_E): the status becomes suspended, not negated by fiat.
7. Since E is suspended, `remote_open_command` remains D-gated.
8. Later a direct local inspection confirms `closed(V12)`. Now (\mathrm{commit}_{E\to O}) lawfully revises O from `open` to `closed`.

**Witness requirements**

* sensor record,
* camera/image provenance,
* inspection log,
* calibration/provenance downgrade record,
* derivation witness linking each source to the claim.

**Blocked alternative**

Directly treating the early sensor readout as settled O and handing `open(V12)=true` downstream is blocked. That would be weak E laundering into O-certainty.

**Final downstream projection**

Before the inspection closes the conflict, (\Pi_B) for `open(V12)` is not ready. The lawful output is suspension. After revision, (\Pi_B(open(V12))=0).

### Example 2 — empirical/scientific claim from O/E tension to bounded verdict

**Initial mixed state**
Claim: “Drug A reduces blood pressure in adults with condition X.”

* (O): candidate causal relation `reduces_bp(DrugA, X)`.
* (E): one observational study supports it; an RCT supports a smaller effect; subgroup heterogeneity remains open.
* (U): desire to recommend widely because the drug is cheap and available.
* (D): clinical guideline rules not yet triggered.

**Lawful path**

1. (\mathrm{form}_{O\to E}): causal candidate becomes an assessable empirical claim.
2. (\mathrm{acc}_E): observational and RCT evidence are added.
3. (\mathrm{cf}_E): branch by subgroup assumptions, such as age or comorbidity.
4. (\mathrm{weak}_E): broad external-validity claims are weakened because subgroup transport is not yet secured.
5. The universal move (\mathrm{commit}_{E\to O}) to “Drug A reduces BP in all adults with X” is blocked.
6. A narrower move is lawful: commit only the bounded ontic claim “modest reduction under dose (d) in subgroup (X_{<65})” if that is what the evidence actually covers.
7. (\mathrm{upd}_{E\to U}): expected utility of recommending widely is reduced because effect generality is uncertain.

**Witness requirements**

* study reports,
* method-quality/provenance witnesses,
* subgroup definition witness,
* derivation witness from evidence bundle to the narrowed claim.

**Blocked alternative**

A cost-driven U preference for broad recommendation cannot convert the narrowed evidence into a broad O claim.

**Final downstream projection**

A lawful downstream output is probabilistic or interval-valued, for example (\Pi_P) over the bounded subgroup. A universal Boolean efficacy verdict is not yet ready.

### Example 3 — governance/compliance: E grounds D

**Initial mixed state**
Incident (I): customer data store exposed.

* (O): event object (I), affected dataset (D_s), temporal duration.
* (E): access logs, asset inventory, classification register, DLP alerts.
* (D): regulation (n): if personal data breach likely to create risk, notify within 72 hours.
* (U): minimize harm, reputational cost, and unnecessary escalation.

**Lawful path**

1. (\mathrm{form}_{O\to E}): create claims `personal_data_involved(I)` and `risk_likely(I)`.
2. (\mathrm{acc}_E): logs and inventory strongly support the first claim; DLP alerts and exposure path support the second.
3. If exfiltration is not proven, that need not matter if the norm turns on likely risk rather than confirmed misuse.
4. (\mathrm{bind}_{E\to D}): evidence grounds applicability of norm (n) for this incident.
5. (\mathrm{bind}_D): `notify_within_72h(I)` becomes active.
6. (\mathrm{constr}_{D\to U}): utility search is now over strategies that include timely notification.

**Witness requirements**

* log provenance,
* dataset classification witness,
* scope witness showing the regulation applies in this jurisdiction and incident class,
* derivation witness from incident facts to norm antecedent.

**Blocked alternative**

U cannot mint a new deontic exemption such as “delay notice because legal review is costly” unless there is a real exception path with authority.

**Final downstream projection**

[
\Pi_D(I)=\mathrm{required}(\text{notify_within_72h})
]

not because utility likes it, but because E lawfully bound an existing D norm.

### Example 4 — strategy case: U active, D-constrained, E-informed

**Initial mixed state**
A medical supply drone must deliver temperature-sensitive payload.

* (O): candidate routes (r_1,r_2,r_3).
* (E): weather forecast, battery model, uncertainty about wind corridor.
* (D): no-fly zones, maximum risk threshold, cold-chain duration requirement.
* (U): minimize delivery time and maximize arrival reliability.

**Lawful path**

1. (\mathrm{target}_{U\to O}): routes are generated as candidate O-plans, not facts.
2. (\mathrm{eval}_{O\to U}): each route is scored for time/cost/reliability.
3. (\mathrm{upd}_{E\to U}): forecast uncertainty revises expected values.
4. (\mathrm{inst}_{O\to D}): route geometry instantiates which regulations and spatial prohibitions apply.
5. (\mathrm{constr}*{D\to U}): route set is filtered to (A*{\mathrm{adm}}).
6. (\mathrm{opt}_U^B): bounded optimization selects the best admissible route.

**Witness requirements**

* map and route geometry,
* no-fly and regulatory authority sources,
* forecast and battery provenance,
* derivation witness for utility scores.

**Blocked alternative**

(r_1) is shortest but crosses restricted airspace. It remains utility-attractive but deontically inadmissible, so it is removed from the feasible set.

**Final downstream projection**

(\Pi_U) returns the best route among (A_{\mathrm{adm}}), not the globally shortest route.

### Example 5 — exception/escalation case: U cannot launder a D override

**Initial mixed state**
A critical production outage is ongoing.

* (O): service degradation event `SEV1`.
* (E): telemetry, blast radius estimate, rollback package, incomplete root-cause certainty.
* (D): normal rule says “two approvals and tests before production deploy.” Exception rule says break-glass is allowed only if `SEV1`, rollback is prepared, and incident commander approves.
* (U): restore service immediately.

**Lawful path**

1. (\mathrm{form}_{O\to E}): create assessable claim `SEV1(active)`.
2. (\mathrm{acc}_E): telemetry and incident logs support the severity claim; rollback package witness supports readiness.
3. (\mathrm{pet}_{U\to D}): utility pressure creates a break-glass request.
4. If incident commander approval is missing, (\mathrm{exc}_D) cannot bind. The lawful output is `escalate` or `block`, not “permission by urgency.”
5. If the commander signs and the exception conditions are all witnessed, then (\mathrm{exc}_D) locally binds the exception for this incident.
6. (\mathrm{constr}_{D\to U}): utility search now runs over the break-glass admissible actions only.

**Witness requirements**

* severity telemetry,
* rollback readiness,
* exception rule reference,
* authority witness for incident commander,
* scope witness that the exception applies only to this incident/time window.

**Blocked alternative**

“Restoring service is so important that we are automatically allowed to bypass approvals” is illicit U-to-D laundering.

**Final downstream projection**

Before approval:

[
\Pi_D=\mathrm{escalate}
]

After valid approval:

[
\Pi_D=\mathrm{permitted_under_exception}
]

for this scope only, not as a standing new norm.

## 13. Final doctrine summary

1. **ODEU is best formalized as a witness-guarded stratified fibered state-space with partial typed morphisms.**
   That is the weakest object strong enough to capture mixed loading, paired tensions, lawful state change, and bounded downstream projection.

2. **The 2D ODEU cross remains valid, but only as a chart.**
   It preserves first-order dominance in the O/E and D/U pairings. It does not preserve witness structure, authority, path, supersession, or branch geometry.

3. **O, E, D, and U are distinct semantic regimes with distinct operator laws.**
   O carries world-structure; E carries warrant/challenge/suspension; D carries binding force and authority; U carries desirability and bounded search. None of these collapse into one scalar.

4. **Lawful morphisms are partial, typed, and guarded.**
   The right question is never only “can we move from here to there?” It is “what witness bundle, scope closure, provenance quality, and authority context make that move admissible, suspended, escalated, or blocked?”

5. **Witness-bearing transition law is central.**
   Two visually identical endpoints can differ in legitimacy because one is witness-closed and authority-grounded while the other is laundered. The morphism is not fully identified by source and target alone.

6. **The anti-laundering doctrine is strict.**
   U cannot mint D. Weak E cannot masquerade as settled O. O cannot bypass E into downstream closure. Exceptions cannot become norms by convenience. Overrides need a real office.

7. **Path dependence is part of the mathematics, not an implementation detail.**
   Composition is partial. Order matters. Reversibility is conditional. Supersession propagates along dependency paths.

8. **ODEU is a membrane, not a replacement for all downstream logic.**
   It prepares bounded, witness-closed, authority-closed packets for Boolean closure, probabilistic estimation, deontic verdict, and utility selection. Those operators remain real and downstream.

9. **The constitutional sentence is therefore:**

   > ODEU is the last witness-bearing semantic field before bounded adjudication; its law is the law of admissible morphisms, not the law of premature closure.
